##About the author

Stephan Hartgers has spent over 25 years helping organizations create real value from technology, long before AI became a boardroom imperative. He has worked across the full spectrum, from junior consultant to VP, across sectors and continents, with clients ranging from leading institutions to small startups, across Europe, North-America, China, Middle-East and Australia.

His career spans McKinsey, where he led high-stakes transformation programs, to senior leadership roles at digital agency Mobiquity, where he served clients across the globe, leading a growing consulting and product management practise. He created the digital innovation framework and later the AI-first approach to software development, and guided executive teams through digital transformation. He holds a Master of Science in Econometrics from the University of Maastricht and completed leadership programs at INSEAD.

Today, through his independent advisory practice, Stephan works with CEOs, CIOs, and leadership teams navigating the transition to an AI-shaped world. His premise is simple: AI is no longer a technology question. It is a fundamental leadership question. And most organizations are not yet asking the right ones.

The Agentic Enterprise is the distillation of those years in the field, the patterns he has seen, the failures he has witnessed, and the handful of organizations that got it right. It is written for leaders who want strategic clarity, not hype, and who understand that the real work of AI transformation happens in the organization, not the algorithm.

Stephan is married and lives in The Hague with his two daughters. When he is not advising companies, you will likely find him on the water, kite-surfing.
