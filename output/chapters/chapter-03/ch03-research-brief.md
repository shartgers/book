   - Source: EU AI Act Article 4, via research/ch3 file Section 6
   - Use for: Beat 4 — EU AI Act maps directly onto the governance of agentic employees; AI literacy = Tier 1 regulatory floor

2. **Germany's co-determination (Mitbestimmung) obligation** — The Works Constitution Act requires employers to inform works councils before introducing AI systems (§90), grants works councils the right to consult experts (§80), and triggers co-determination when AI can monitor employee behaviour (§87). Deutsche Telekom's AI Manifesto, co-created with the Group Works Council, demonstrates that institutionalised stakeholder engagement produces faster, more durable adoption.
   - Source: German Works Constitution Act; Deutsche Telekom example, research/ch3 file Section 6

   - Use for: Beat 4 — concrete legal risk from governance failure in Europe

## The Agentic Employee — Definition, Profiles, and Mindset Shift

This section is the primary source material for Beat 3 and should be reproduced in the brief in full because the Writer needs every element.

### Definition (from research/the-agentic-employee.md)

An agentic employee is a person who:
1. Works alongside AI agents — autonomous systems that can plan, decide, and execute multi-step tasks independently.
2. Orchestrates rather than executes — sets goals, defines boundaries, reviews outputs, and handles exceptions, while agents handle routine and complex execution.
3. Creates and shares reusable AI workflows — builds skills, prompts, and processes that multiply their own impact and the impact of their team.
4. Exercises judgment at the right moments — knows when to trust agent outputs, when to intervene, and when to escalate.

The term "agentic" itself means "capable of achieving outcomes independently" (Moveworks, 2025). Applied to employees, it describes a person who is empowered — through AI — to achieve far more than they could alone, while maintaining accountability for outcomes.

### Three Profiles (CognitionHub, 2026)

| Profile | Role | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Supervisor** | Orchestrates multiple AI agents across workflows | A project manager who coordinates AI agents handling scheduling, reporting, and resource allocation |
| **Specialist** | Handles exceptions and edge cases that agents cannot resolve | A compliance officer who reviews flagged transactions that agents escalated |
| **Augmented Frontline Worker** | Uses AI agents to eliminate repetitive tasks and focus on human-centric work | A customer success rep whose agent handles data gathering, letting them focus on relationship-building |

### Mindset Shift Table (from research/the-agentic-employee.md)

| Old Mindset | Agentic Mindset |
|-------------|-----------------|
| "I do the work" | "I orchestrate the work" |
| "AI is a tool I use sometimes" | "AI agents are teammates I collaborate with constantly" |
| "My value is what I produce" | "My value is the outcomes I enable" |
| "I learn skills to stay relevant" | "I create and share skills to multiply impact" |
| "I follow processes" | "I design and improve processes" |
| "I need instructions" | "I set goals and boundaries for agents, then review results" |

Source: research/the-agentic-employee.md Section 4

### A Skill — Definition

A **skill** is a reusable AI workflow — a discrete, repeatable sequence of agent actions built by an employee and shared across the organisation. The best agentic employees do not just use AI; they create skills and share them, producing a compounding network effect where one person's innovation multiplies the capability of everyone around them.
- Forrester defines the dual identity: Agent as a Skill (discrete cognitive capability) and Agent as a Product (platform-resident with governance and telemetry).
- Source: book-research-claude.md Section 1 (Forrester); research/the-agentic-employee.md Section 2.2

### Human-in-the-Loop — Concept

Human-in-the-loop means designing human checkpoints at high-stakes decision points — rather than throughout every step — in a human-agent workflow. Governance elements: tiered autonomy (risk-adjusted boundaries), comprehensive logging (every agent action captured), and circuit breakers (real-time monitoring that can halt agent actions).
- Source: research/the-agentic-employee.md Section 3.3

### Productivity Multiplier Evidence

- An SSRN study (Sarkar, November 2025) found software output increased by 39% after AI agents became the default platform. Crucially, more experienced workers benefit more from agents (one standard deviation higher experience = 6% higher acceptance rates), while junior workers benefit more from tool-only AI.
- Stanford HAI and MIT CSAIL studies show time savings of 65–86% versus human-only workflows.
- A legal technology trial across 12 firms found a 41% average productivity increase.
- Source: research/ch3 file Section 4

## Case Study Data — Klarna (Sweden)

### Company context
Swedish fintech; CEO-driven AI-first transformation from the top.

### Dilemma
Klarna pursued aggressive AI-first automation under direct CEO leadership, achieving dramatic efficiency gains, then discovered the limits of automation for complex, emotional customer interactions.

### Decision made
CEO Sebastian Siemiatkowski drove deployment of an AI assistant handling customer service at scale. Following metrics shortfalls in customer satisfaction for complex cases, Klarna partially reversed course, re-hiring human agents for a hybrid model.

### Outcomes and metrics
- AI assistant handled **2.3 million conversations in its first month** — equivalent of 700 full-time agents
- Resolution time fell from **11 minutes to under 2 minutes**
- Projected **$40 million in annual profit improvement**
- Klarna's Agentic Product Protocol launched: an open standard making over 100 million products discoverable by AI agents across 12 markets
- AI-driven dynamic "lookbooks" with Google Cloud boosted in-app time by **15%** and orders by **50%**
- **86% of customers prefer empathy over speed** for complex issues
- Customer satisfaction declined for high-complexity, emotionally charged cases
- Workforce cut by approximately 40%; then reversed, re-hiring humans
- Siemiatkowski's own admission: "cost unfortunately seems to have been a too predominant evaluation factor"

### Key quote
Sebastian Siemiatkowski (Klarna CEO): *"AI gives us speed. Talent gives us empathy."*

### Strategic lesson for this chapter
Klarna's story illustrates both the power and the limits of the Agentic Organisation model. The destination is not full automation but a deliberately designed hybrid — humans and agents each doing what they do best. The hybrid model is the endgame; the AI Transformation Framework's four layers (Vision, Products, Operating Model, Foundation) provide the structure for designing it intentionally rather than discovering it by reversal.

Sources: book-research-claude.md Section 7 (Klarna); research/ch3 file Sections 2 and 5

## Governance Evidence (Beat 4)

1. **Board governance gap** — 66% of boards report limited, minimal, or no AI knowledge. Only 15% of boards receive AI-related metrics. Fewer than 25% have board-approved AI policies. Only 14% of boards discuss AI at every meeting. Yet 88% of organisations use AI in at least one business function.
   - Source: book-research-claude.md Section 9

2. **Governance gap in agentic deployment** — Only 21% of organisations have rigorous governance mechanisms in place, even as 57% have agents running in production (Deloitte, 2026; Towards Agentic AI, 2026).
   - Use for: Beat 4 — headline governance gap statistic

3. **Critical governance elements for agentic employees** (Deloitte, 2026):
   - Tiered autonomy: risk-adjusted boundaries — agents handle low-risk decisions independently, escalate high-impact actions to humans
   - Comprehensive logging: capture every agent action for inspection, audit, and compliance
   - Circuit breakers: real-time monitoring that enforces boundaries and can halt agent actions
   - Pre-deployment assessment: data permissions, system access, risk classification
   - Shadow agent policy: address employees quietly deploying unapproved autonomous tools without IT oversight
   - Source: research/the-agentic-employee.md Section 3.3

4. **EU AI Act obligations map to framework dimensions** — For European organisations:
   - AI literacy (People layer): Article 4, in force February 2025
   - Risk classification (Process/Technology layers): high-risk obligations active August 2026
   - Data protection (Data layer): GDPR + AI Act co-obligations for automated processing
   - Product compliance (Products layer): transparency requirements for AI-enabled products
   - Source: EU AI Act; research/ch3 file Section 6

5. **McKinsey board governance recommendation** — Boards should determine their "AI posture," codify governance frameworks, and demand specific metrics: ROI by business unit, % of processes AI-enabled, workforce reskilling progress, regulatory alignment, override rates.
   - Source: book-research-claude.md Section 9
