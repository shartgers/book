### Beat 1 — The delegation trap

- **Claim:** Bain & Company coined the "micro-productivity trap" — a proliferation of proofs of concept and isolated use cases that deliver modest, localized efficiency gains but fail to scale. Their verdict is unequivocal: *"Grassroots experimentation with AI tools sparks innovation and cultural momentum — all good — but it does not self-organize into enterprise-wide impact. Without clear direction from the top, these efforts remain fragmented, siloed, and ultimately shallow."*
  — Source: Bain & Company (research synthesis)

- **Claim:** 90% of transformative AI use cases remain stuck in pilot mode. McKinsey's June 2025 CEO Playbook identifies the "Gen AI Paradox": nearly 8 in 10 companies report using gen AI, yet just as many report no significant bottom-line impact.
  — Source: McKinsey, June 2025 CEO Playbook

- **Claim:** S&P Global reports 42% of companies scrapped most AI initiatives in 2025, up from 17% the year before — a more than doubling of abandonment in a single year.
  — Source: S&P Global (research synthesis)

- **Claim:** The RAND Corporation puts AI project failure at over 80% — double non-AI IT failure rates. MIT NANDA Report (2025): 95% of enterprise generative AI implementations fail to deliver sustained, measurable impact.
  — Source: RAND Corporation; MIT NANDA Report 2025

## European Context

### EU AI Act — leadership accountability implications

- **Claim:** The EU AI Act creates direct obligations on organisational leadership. Prohibited-risk provisions applied **2 February 2025**; GPAI transparency requirements took effect **2 August 2025**; full high-risk system requirements apply **2 August 2026** (potentially extended to December 2027 via the Digital Omnibus). Penalties reach **€35 million or 7% of global annual turnover**.
  — Source: Research synthesis (Section 6)

- **Claim:** The EU AI Act requires organisations to: complete an AI inventory with risk classification, clarify supply chain role, implement risk management systems, establish AI literacy training (already required since February 2025), and conduct fundamental rights impact assessments. These are leadership-level decisions, not IT tasks.
  — Source: Research synthesis (Section 6)

- **Implication for chapter:** The EU AI Act is a structural reason why delegation fails in Europe specifically. Compliance accountability cannot be delegated to IT; it sits with the board and executive team. The Act therefore reinforces rather than complicates the chapter's central argument.

### European board readiness gap

- **Claim:** European insurers lead in responsible AI talent, accounting for **47% of specialists in data ethics, governance, and risk** despite representing 36% of total AI talent — but this is a function-level capability, not board-level governance.
  — Source: Research synthesis (Section 6)

- **Claim:** Over **60% of European firms are still at the earliest stages** of AI maturity (Deloitte / AI Readiness Index). Western Europe scores 69.56 on the AI Readiness Index (2nd globally), but this masks a governance maturity gap — the board knowledge deficit is consistent across geographies.
  — Source: AI Readiness Index 2024 (research synthesis, Section 9)

- **Note for Writer:** No specific EU-sourced data on board AI knowledge gaps exists in the research folder beyond the Deloitte 66% figure (which is global). The Deloitte statistic is authoritative enough to stand; qualify it as a global finding that applies acutely in European governance structures given EU AI Act accountability obligations.

## Case Study Data — DBS Bank

**Assigned to:** Case Study Agent. All data below is for the Case Study Agent's use. Writer leaves `<!-- CASE STUDY PLACEHOLDER -->` in the draft.

### Company overview
- **Full name:** DBS Bank (Development Bank of Singapore)
- **CEO:** Piyush Gupta (led transformation personally from inception)
- **Location:** Singapore — relevant as a non-European case study that demonstrates universally applicable leadership principles; strongest available global proof point for CEO-led transformation

### The dilemma they faced
- DBS was known internally and externally as "Damn Bloody Slow" — a legacy bank struggling with the reputation of bureaucracy and slow digital delivery
- The strategic challenge: how to transform a large, established bank into a technology-forward institution without being disrupted by fintech challengers or big tech entrants
- The decision point: whether to delegate digital/AI transformation to a technology function, or for the CEO to personally own and drive it

### Decision made
- CEO Piyush Gupta personally led the transformation — not as a sponsor, but as an active, accountable driving force
- Introduced the **GANDALF framework**: benchmarking DBS not against other banks, but against **Google, Amazon, Netflix, Apple, LinkedIn, Facebook** — a deliberate reframing of competitive peer set
- The GANDALF framework forced the organisation to think at technology-company standards of speed, data use, and customer experience rather than banking-industry norms

### Outcomes and metrics
- AI economic value grew from **S$180 million (2022) to S$750 million (2024)**, with S$1 billion projected for 2025
- **Share price tripled** over the transformation period
- **Return on Equity (ROE) doubled** from 8.4% to 18% — a sustained financial performance improvement, not a one-off cost reduction
- DBS is one of **only two banks globally** (alongside JPMorgan Chase) to have publicly disclosed total realised AI value — a transparency and governance signal in itself

### Key signals of CEO-led accountability (for case study narrative use)
- The GANDALF framing came from Gupta personally — it was a CEO-level act of strategic reframing, not a branding exercise
- The consistent public disclosure of AI value metrics (S$180M → S$750M) reflects a governance discipline that begins at the top — boards demand what CEOs model
- The transformation moved from digital banking to AI-native operations as a continuous programme, not a sequence of projects — continuity driven by sustained CEO engagement
