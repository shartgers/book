# Chapter 05 — The AI Transformation Framework

## Strategic Tension

Every transformation needs a map. Without one, pilots scatter, investments fragment, and the board has no shared language for "where we are" and "what we must build." The tension is between the complexity of AI transformation—touching products, people, process, technology, data, governance—and the need for a single, visualisable framework that every leader can use to orient themselves and the rest of the book. This chapter provides that map: the AI Transformation Framework. It does not deep-dive; it orients. Later chapters take each dimension further.

## Core Principle

The AI Transformation Framework is the map for the Agentic Organisation: four layers—Vision, Products, Operating Model, and Foundation—that define what the organisation is made of and how to assess readiness; every layer must be addressed, and the framework image is the reader's reference for the rest of the book. Governance, KPIs, and the Value/Capabilities/Trust decision framework are covered elsewhere (Ch 8, Ch 12–13) but are not part of the four-layer framework.

## Named Framework

**The AI Transformation Framework** — Four layers (top to bottom), as in images/ai-transformation-framework.png: (1) Vision — vision, strategy, values, objectives; (2) Products — by platform or business unit; product vision & objectives, products, personas, customer journeys, frictions; (3) Operating Model — People: roles, target skills & gaps, org sizing, talent journey, training, team incentives; Process: org design, cross-functional teams, product way-of-working; (4) Foundation — Data: reusable data products, analytics, architecture, monetization; Technology: target architecture, platforms & solutions, integration; Enablement: culture, AI & engineering toolbox, automation, agents, skills. Use when describing what the Agentic Organisation is made of or how to assess readiness. Must match the framework image. Do not refer to Value/Capabilities/Trust or governance/KPIs/implementation as framework layers.

### Beat 2 — The Problem

Section heading: Why a Single Framework Matters

Why scattered initiatives fail: no shared model of what the organisation is building. McKinsey's Gen AI Paradox (eight in ten use gen AI, eight in ten report no significant bottom-line impact); 90% of transformative use cases stuck in pilot. Deloitte: 80% of AI deployment effort consumed by "unglamorous tasks"—data engineering, stakeholder alignment, governance, workflow integration. Only 12% of organisations have AI-ready data (WEF). The problem is not technology adoption but the absence of a coherent, multi-dimensional model. The framework gives the board and C-suite a shared vocabulary: the four layers from Vision to Foundation. It is the basis for "where are we, and where do we need to be?" on each layer.

### Beat 4 — Governance Implications

Section heading: The Framework as a Governance Instrument

The framework is the basis for board and C-suite conversations: "Where are we on each layer? Where do we need to be?" Governance demands span all four layers, not just technology. Only 21% of organisations have rigorous AI governance (Deloitte). For European organisations, EU AI Act obligations map onto the framework: AI literacy (Operating Model), risk classification (Technology/Enablement in Foundation), data protection (Data), product compliance (Products), and the trust outcomes (Value, Capabilities, Trust—covered in Ch 8 as a decision framework, not a layer). The board should assess transformation progress against each layer rather than relying on pilot reports. The framework also connects to the Four Tiers (Ch 4): each tier requires progress across the four layers; skipping a tier or a layer creates blind spots.

### Beat 5 — Closing / Transition

Section heading: From Map to Execution

The AI Transformation Framework provides the map. The next question is how to organise the organisation for agentic work: roles, skills, team design, and the Operating Model layer that connects vision to day-to-day execution. Chapter 6 deep-dives the Operating Model layer—people, process, roles, skills, org design—and how to structure teams and roles for agentic work, from task-doer to orchestrator at scale. The framework orients; the Operating Model layer is where strategy becomes structure and where many organisations bottleneck after successful pilots.

### Beat 6 — Executive Reflection Questions

Section heading: Questions for the Board — The Framework

1. Can your leadership team name the four layers of the AI Transformation Framework (Vision, Products, Operating Model, Foundation) and describe where your organisation stands on each?
2. Which layer is most advanced in your organisation, and which is most neglected? What is the consequence of that imbalance?
3. Does your board have a structured way to assess AI transformation progress across all four layers, or does it rely on technology metrics and pilot updates?
4. How does your AI strategy explicitly address each of the four layers—and how do you use the Value/Capabilities/Trust decision framework (Ch 8) and governance/KPIs (Ch 12–13) to prioritise and measure?
5. When you look at your portfolio of AI initiatives, do they map coherently onto the framework, or are they concentrated in one or two layers with gaps elsewhere?
