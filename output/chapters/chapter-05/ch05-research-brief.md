# Research Brief — Chapter 05 — The AI Transformation Framework

## Framework Evidence

- **Four-layer structure (canonical):** Vision, Products, Operating Model, Foundation — per `docs/instructions.md` and `plan/concept.md`. Match `images/ai-transformation-framework.png`. Do not refer to Value/Capabilities/Trust or governance/KPIs as framework layers.
- **McKinsey "The Agentic Organization" (Sept 2025):** "The largest organizational paradigm shift since the industrial and digital revolutions." Defines organisation uniting humans and AI agents at scale at near-zero marginal cost. Five pillars in their model: Business Model, Operating Model, Governance, Workforce/People/Culture, Technology/Data — book uses four-layer framework; Vision maps to strategy, Products to business model, Operating Model to people/process, Foundation to technology/data.
- **Scattered initiatives fail:** No shared model of what the organisation is building. McKinsey State of AI: only **5.5% of respondents attribute more than 5% of EBIT to AI** (2025). BCG: **74% of companies have yet to show tangible value**, only 4% achieving enterprise-wide AI at scale (Oct 2024, 1,000 C-level). MIT NANDA: **95% of generative AI pilots deliver zero measurable return** (300+ initiatives, 52 orgs). Pattern: gap between pilot and production is where value dies.
- **90% of transformative use cases stuck in pilot** — common industry and consulting framing; pilots scatter without a shared transformation map.
- **Deloitte:** Large share of AI deployment effort consumed by "unglamorous" work — data engineering, stakeholder alignment, governance, workflow integration; only **21% of organisations have rigorous AI governance** (Deloitte). Governance demands span all dimensions, not just technology.
- **Data readiness:** World Economic Forum: **fewer than 1 in 5 organisations have high maturity in any aspect of data readiness**; 72% of business leaders prioritise data foundations as fastest-growing AI investment. Gartner: **57% estimate their data is not AI-ready**. Only **12% of organisations globally** report truly AI-ready data (WEF / industry surveys).
- **Workflow redesign:** McKinsey: of 25 organisational attributes tested, **workflow redesign showed the biggest correlation with EBIT impact** from generative AI — yet only 21% of organisations have fundamentally redesigned workflows. Implication: a coherent multi-layer model (Vision → Products → Operating Model → Foundation) is the basis for "where are we, where do we need to be?"
- **Readiness and governance:** Cisco AI Readiness Index (six pillars: Strategy, Infrastructure, Data, Governance, Talent, Culture). Over **60% of European firms at earliest stages** of AI maturity. **66% of boards report limited to no AI knowledge**; only 15% receive AI-related metrics (McKinsey). Board should assess transformation against each layer, not just pilot reports.

## European Context

- **EU AI Act:** In force Aug 2024; **prohibitions and AI literacy requirements from Feb 2025**. High-risk obligations and majority of Act from **Aug 2026**. Risk classification: unacceptable (banned), high-risk, limited, minimal. Penalties up to **€35 million or 7% of global annual turnover**. For European organisations, framework maps to obligations: AI literacy (Operating Model), risk classification (Technology/Enablement in Foundation), data protection (Data in Foundation), product compliance (Products). Value, Capabilities, Trust is a decision framework (Ch 8), not a layer.
- **Western Europe AI Readiness Index 2024:** 69.56 (2nd after North America 82.60). France 79.36, UK 78.88, Netherlands 77.23, Germany 76.90, Finland 76.48. Majority of firms still at early maturity.
- **Board governance:** Only 21% of organisations have rigorous AI governance (Deloitte). Framework gives board and C-suite shared vocabulary to assess progress across all four layers.

## Case Study Data — BBVA

- **Context:** Major European bank; enterprise-wide AI transformation using a single, coherent map (the AI Transformation Framework in practice).
- **Scale:** **120,000 employees** across **25 countries**. Strategic alliance with OpenAI (ChatGPT Enterprise).
- **Engagement:** **80% daily engagement**; employees saving **~3 hours per week**. **3,000+ custom bots** created.
- **Structure:** "AI Factory" of **400+ professionals**. **300 embedded "AI wizards"** in business units. Illustrates addressing all four layers (Vision, Products, Operating Model, Foundation) under one vision, alongside governance and the Value/Capabilities/Trust decision framework.
- **Leadership:** Chair Carlos Torres Vila: ambition to enter the AI era "with even greater ambition" than digital and mobile transformation. Framework as organising principle for scale, not a checklist.
- **Dilemma (for case study):** How does a major European bank deploy a single, enterprise-wide AI transformation map across 120,000 employees in 25 countries?
