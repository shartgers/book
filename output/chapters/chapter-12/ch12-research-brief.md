# Research Brief — Chapter 12 — Readiness

## Framework Evidence

1. **Only 13–14% of organisations qualify as "Pacesetters"** — Cisco AI Readiness Index (2024–2025, 8,000+ leaders, 30 markets). Pacesetters are 4x more likely to have finalised AI use cases (77% vs ~19%), 3x more likely to track AI investment impact (95% vs 32%), and report 1.5x higher gains in profitability, productivity, and innovation. Pacesetter profile: 99% have well-defined strategy (vs 58% overall), 76% have centralised data (vs 19%), 84% have end-to-end governance with continuous monitoring (vs 30%). — Source: Cisco AI Readiness Index 2025, cisco.com

2. **The stage 2→3 transition is the greatest financial inflection point** — MIT CISR Enterprise AI Maturity Model (721 companies, 9 case studies). Companies in stages 1–2 (ad hoc pilots) perform below industry average financially; stages 3–4 (scaled AI ways of working) perform well above. Crossing from "building pilots" to "scaled AI ways of working" delivers the largest jump in financial performance. — Source: MIT CISR 2024–2025 (Weill, Woerner, Sebastian, Kaganer)

3. **AI maturity is getting harder, not easier** — ServiceNow Enterprise AI Maturity Index (2025, ~4,500 executives, 16 countries). Average maturity scores declined 9 points year-over-year (20% drop). Fewer than 1% scored above 50 on a 100-point scale. Estimated value if all Forbes Global 2000 achieved Pacesetter maturity: $113 billion in aggregate gross margin increase. — Source: ServiceNow 2025, servicenow.com

4. **Strategy-operations gap is the readiness gap in disguise** — Deloitte State of AI (2026). 42% of companies believe their strategy is highly prepared for AI — but feel significantly less prepared operationally on infrastructure, data, risk, and talent. Only 21% have a mature governance model for autonomous AI agents, despite 74% planning agentic AI deployment within two years. Only 25% have moved 40%+ of pilots into production. — Source: Deloitte 2026, deloitte.com

5. **Workflow redesign and CEO oversight are the highest-impact readiness fixes** — McKinsey State of AI (2025). Out of 25 organisational attributes tested, redesigning workflows has the biggest effect on capturing EBIT value from gen AI — yet only 21% of organisations have done this. CEO oversight of AI governance shows the strongest correlation with bottom-line EBIT impact — yet only 28% of organisations have it; only 17% have board-level oversight. — Source: McKinsey 2025, mckinsey.com

## European Context

- **Only 18% of European organisations report being highly prepared** in gen AI risk and governance areas. Only 36% of German organisations report preparedness for EU AI Act implementation. — Deloitte 2026
- **EU AI Act creates specific readiness obligations:** five-phase compliance framework — (1) AI system inventory, (2) risk classification (four tiers), (3) gap analysis, (4) remediation planning, (5) evidence documentation. Key deadline: **August 2026** for high-risk AI system requirements. Penalties: up to **€35 million or 7%** of global annual turnover for prohibited practices; €15 million or 3% for high-risk violations. — EU AI Act / Glocert 2024–2026
- **Western Europe scores 69.56** on Cisco AI Readiness Index (2nd globally after North America at 82.60). France leads at 79.36, followed by UK (78.88), Netherlands (77.23), Germany (76.90), Finland (76.48). Over 60% of European firms are still at the earliest stages of AI maturity. — Cisco 2025
- **74% of European SaaS companies have initiated AI projects, but only 14% have reached full production** with measurable revenue or cost savings. — Lleverage (150+ European tech leaders, 2025)
- **Compliance as trust accelerator:** Allianz signed the EU AI Pact (November 2024) — proactive compliance as competitive moat. European insurers lead globally in responsible AI talent: 47% of specialists in data ethics, governance, and risk despite representing 36% of total AI talent. — Section 6 / research folder
- **Data readiness benchmark:** Gartner finds 57% of organisations globally estimate their data is not AI-ready. WEF: fewer than 1 in 5 organisations have high maturity in any aspect of data readiness. — Gartner / WEF

## Case Study Data — Kore.ai (Global Bank Wealth Management)

- **Dilemma:** A leading global financial services company needed to deploy agentic AI across 120,000 employees and 80,000 wealth advisors without the "big bang" approach that kills most enterprise transformations. Advisors spent hours searching fragmented systems; generic AI tools were insufficient for client-facing compliance work.
- **Decision:** Readiness-gated, phased deployment using Kore.ai's AI for Work platform, with each phase serving as a readiness checkpoint before advancing:
  - **Phase 1 — Content creation** (lowest risk, highest immediate value): Email Wizard, Marketing Muse, Summarizer, meeting transcription.
  - **Phase 2 — Client management** (higher complexity, data integration readiness required): Meeting Prep consolidating email/CRM/portfolio data; Estate Planning analyzer; Asset Verification Letters.
  - **Phase 3 — Compliance and governance** (highest trust requirements, deployed only after governance infrastructure proven): CRC Pre-Review evaluates communications against regulatory requirements; built-in compliance checks.
  - **Phase 4 — Portfolio management and investment intelligence:** Financial Advisor Super Agent, ESG Due Diligence, Investment Advisor, Deep Research.
  - **Phase 5 — Employee productivity:** Goal-Setting Assistant for performance management.
- **Orchestration:** A "supervisor agent" managed handoffs between phases. 2,600+ agents built by employees using low-code-to-pro-code builder.
- **Outcomes:**
  - Meeting prep time: **2–3 hours → 15 minutes**
  - Document findability: **20% → 80%** (300% improvement)
  - Employee satisfaction: **+22%**
  - Task time reduction: **12%**
  - Annual efficiency savings: **over $5 million**
  - Hours saved: **90,000+**
  - AI interactions: **80,000 daily**
- **Readiness Gate connection:** Each phase diagnosed capability at the current level, prioritised the next deployment based on proven readiness (value, capability, trust), and sequenced rollout so each phase built the foundation for the next. Compliance (Phase 3) was gated behind governance readiness; portfolio intelligence (Phase 4) behind data integration readiness.
- **Sources:** Kore.ai customer stories (kore.ai/customer-stories/global-bank; kore.ai/customer-stories/global-bank-financial-advisor-ai-support); Kore.ai blog

## Additional Evidence for Specific Beats

### Beat 1 (Opening Hook — The Thirteen Per Cent Club)
- Cisco: 13–14% Pacesetters across 49 indicators, 6 pillars. The remaining 86% are not failing at technology — they are failing at knowing where they stand.
- MIT CISR: Stage 2→3 = greatest financial inflection. You cannot cross a threshold you cannot see.

### Beat 2 (The Readiness Paradox — two failure modes)
- **Failure mode 1 (scale prematurely):** BCG: 60% of companies fail to define or monitor any financial KPIs for AI value creation; spread investments broadly → lower ROI. S&P Global: 42% scrapped most AI initiatives in 2025, up from 17%. 95% failure rate (MIT NANDA 2025).
- **Failure mode 2 (over-assess, never start):** BCG Trailblazers (~15%) reject both modes — invest in readiness and deployment simultaneously; 94% continue investment regardless of immediate returns; spend 8+ hours weekly on personal AI training.
- **Most common European readiness gaps:** (1) Data governance: 57% not AI-ready (Gartner); (2) Governance: 18% highly prepared in Europe (Deloitte); (3) Talent: <1 in 5 smaller orgs have required talent; only 6% begun meaningful upskilling (BCG/McKinsey); (4) Pilot-to-production: 74% initiated, 14% in production (Lleverage).

### Beat 3 (The Readiness Gate — framework introduction)
- **Step 1 Diagnose:** Use AI Transformation Framework's four layers (Vision, Products, Operating Model, Foundation) + governance and culture. Cisco Pacesetter benchmarks for comparison (99% strategy, 76% data, 84% governance).
- **Step 2 Prioritise:** Use AI Decision Framework (Value, Capabilities, Trust) to rank gaps. McKinsey: workflow redesign = biggest EBIT effect (21% done); CEO oversight = strongest EBIT correlation (28% done). These are high-value, high-trust, feasible gaps.
- **Step 3 Sequence:** Map to Ch 11 playbook phases. Foundation → Activation gate: data accessible, governance for pilots, sponsor assigned, 1–2 use cases with metrics. Activation → Scale gate: pilot results, operating model functioning, >30% upskilled, governance scales.
- **Recurring checkpoint:** Run quarterly or at phase transitions. Pacesetters are 3x more likely to track AI investment impact (95% vs 32% — Cisco).

### Beat 4 (Compliance as a Readiness Dimension)
- EU AI Act five-phase compliance: inventory → classify → gap analysis → remediate → evidence pack. August 2026 high-risk deadline.
- Only 36% German orgs prepared (Deloitte); only 18% European orgs highly prepared on governance.
- Allianz: EU AI Pact signatory (Nov 2024) — proactive compliance as trust accelerator.
- European insurers: 47% of responsible AI specialists despite 36% of total AI talent.
- Penalties: €35M or 7% turnover (prohibited); €15M or 3% (high-risk).
