# Research Brief — Chapter 04 — The Four Tiers of Transformation

## Framework Evidence

### Individual (Tier 1)

- **McKinsey "Superagency" (Jan 2025):** 91% of employees already use generative AI at work, but only 1% of companies report AI maturity — individual adoption massively outpaces organisational readiness. (Already cited in Ch 1/2; reference without full re-explanation.)
- **4D AI Fluency Framework** (Professors Rick Dakan and Joseph Feller, University College Cork, in collaboration with Anthropic): AI fluency defined as "the ability to work effectively, efficiently, ethically, and safely within emerging modalities of Human-AI interaction." Built on four competencies: Delegation (knowing what to assign to AI), Description (communicating effectively with AI), Discernment (evaluating outputs critically), Diligence (maintaining ethical standards). 24 observable behaviours. Augmentative conversations — treating AI as a thinking partner — exhibit more than double the fluency behaviours compared to transactional interactions.
- **Champion programmes:** Citi built a network of 4,000+ "AI Accelerators" across 182,000 employees in 84 countries, reaching 70%+ adoption. PwC Netherlands scaled from 300 AI enthusiasts to all 6,000 employees in roughly one year using organisational network analysis to identify influential people as champions. HSBC launched a five-tier AI Ambassador Network globally. Adobe selected 50 ambassadors across every geography and role.
- **Prompt libraries:** Companies using structured prompt libraries report teams becoming 40% more productive by eliminating redundant prompt creation; organisations with these libraries achieve 85% employee AI adoption vs 23% for individual-only usage.
- **Harvard/BCG "Jagged Frontier" study** (758 BCG consultants, 2023): Participants using GPT-4 completed 12.2% more tasks, finished 25.1% faster, and produced over 40% higher quality results. Below-average performers improved by 43%. Caution: for tasks outside AI's capability frontier, consultants using AI were 19 percentage points less likely to produce correct solutions — critical validation finding.
- **Stanford/MIT customer support study** (5,179 agents, Fortune 500 firm): 14% average productivity increase; 34% improvement for novice workers. Workers with 2 months' AI experience performed as well as workers with 6 months' experience without AI. Manager escalations declined 25%.
- **GitHub Copilot** (Microsoft controlled studies): developers completing tasks 55.8% faster; 12.9–21.8% more pull requests per week.

### Team (Tier 2)

- **MIT Technology Review/Infosys survey** (500 business leaders, December 2025): 83% of executives say psychological safety measurably improves AI initiative success; 84% report direct links to tangible business outcomes. Only 39% rate their organisation's psychological safety as "very high." 22% of leaders admit they have hesitated to lead an AI project for fear of blame if it fails.
- **Slack Workforce Index** (Fall 2024): 48% of workers uncomfortable admitting AI use to manager, fearing they will appear "incompetent, lazy, or cheating." Companies with explicit AI permissions see workers 6× more likely to experiment; trained workers up to 19× more likely to report productivity improvements.
- **Amy Edmondson** (HBR, February 2026, co-authored): "AI adoption should be treated as a team development effort, not just a technology upgrade." (Thinkers50 #2, 2025; defined psychological safety originally.)
- **Google Project Aristotle** (180+ teams, 2012–2014): Psychological safety is the #1 predictor of team effectiveness, above dependability, structure, meaning, and impact.
- **Typeface Signal Report (2025):** 61% of AI use remains at the individual level without collaborative platforms.
- **Nielsen Norman Group meta-analysis:** Average 66% productivity improvement across customer service (13.8% more inquiries/hour), business writing (59% more documents/hour), programming (126% more projects/week).
- **BearingPoint research:** Middle managers must become "orchestrators of AI-human collaboration"; only 35% of companies have structured change management programmes.
- **Two-in-the-box model:** Used at Sun Life (cross-functional teams of 8–12, co-led by business and technology owners), Intel, and P&G/JLL partnerships. Sun Life CIO Laura Money: these teams "much more productive" because they eliminate IT "throwing technology over the fence."

### Department (Tier 3)

- **McKinsey/QuantumBlack Agentic AI Mesh Architecture:** Composable distributed architecture — from solo copilot tools to multi-agent collaboration to swarms of specialised agents coordinating autonomously. Key structural insight: a human team of two to five people can supervise an "agent factory" of 50–100 specialised agents running an end-to-end process.
- **PwC finance workflow:** Agent 1 extracts invoice, Agent 2 pulls contract, Agent 3 flags discrepancies, Agent 4 drafts resolution email — human intervenes only then. Reduces cycle times by up to 80% whilst improving audit trails.
- **Kore.ai wealth management** (global firm, 120,000 employees): Meeting Prep reduced from 2–3 hours to 15 minutes by consolidating email, CRM, and portfolio data. 300% rise in document findability (20% to 80% accessibility). 22% increase in employee satisfaction. 12% reduction in time spent on information retrieval.
- **Siemens Erlangen Digital Lighthouse Factory:** 100+ AI use cases, 69% boost in productivity, 42% reduction in energy use over four years.
- **Airbus:** Trained 10,000+ engineers on GitHub Copilot; 40% improvement in simulation cycle times.
- **EY European AI Barometer 2025:** 56% of European organisations increased profits or reduced costs through AI; average financial impact €6.24 million per firm.
- **IDC:** For every 33 AI proofs-of-concept launched, only 4 reach production — 88% failure rate at the Tier 2–Tier 3 boundary.
- **O'Reilly 2024:** Only 26% of AI initiatives advanced beyond pilot phase; 74% stalling due to operational and organisational barriers.

### Organisation (Tier 4)

- **McKinsey "Rewired"** (200+ large-scale transformations): Companies captured on average only 31% of expected revenue lift and 25% of expected cost savings. Organisations that invested in cultural change saw 5.3× higher success than those focused only on technology.
- **McKinsey digital-trust leaders** ("Why Digital Trust Truly Matters," 2022, 1,333 executives, 27 industries): Digital-trust leaders 1.6× more likely than global average to see revenue and EBIT growth of at least 10%.
- **CoE evolution:** Centralised (strong governance, risk of bottlenecks, first 12 months) → hub-and-spoke (central standards, distributed execution, 12–24 months) → federated (autonomous pods with shared governance, 24–36 months). Already established in 37% of large US companies.
- **BCG:** Active executive sponsors make firms 1.8× more likely to scale AI effectively.
- **McKinsey global retailer example** ("Agents for Growth," November 2025): Demand surges in one region; within seconds, AI agents reallocate ad spend, adjust pricing, reroute stock, refresh creative assets. (Anonymous/illustrative, not a named case study.)
- **European insurer** (McKinsey "Agents for Growth"): AI agents personalising campaigns across hundreds of microsegments produced conversion rates 2–3× higher and 25% shorter call times, deployed in 16 weeks.

### Failure evidence across all tiers

- BCG: 74% of companies struggle to achieve AI value (survey of 1,000 CxOs across 59 countries).
- MIT: 95% of enterprise generative AI pilots fail to deliver measurable returns.
- RAND: AI projects fail at twice the rate of non-AI IT projects (80%+).
- McKinsey: Fewer than 10% of generative AI use cases make it past pilot; over 80% of companies report no material contribution to earnings.
- Common thread: top-down, enterprise-first approaches that bypass individual fluency and team-level norms.

### Leadership at each tier

- **BCG AI Radar 2026** (640 CEOs, 2,360 leaders): C-level executives deeply engaged with AI are 12× more likely to be in the top 5% of companies winning with AI. Only 15% of CEOs qualify as "trailblazers." (Stat already in Ch 2 — reference without full re-introduction.)
- **Tier 1:** CEO as visible AI user; models experimentation; signals psychological safety.
- **Tier 2:** CEO as connector across teams; middle managers as "orchestrators of AI-human collaboration" (BearingPoint); only 35% of companies have structured change management.
- **Tier 3:** CEO as strategist/governor; only 26% of AI initiatives advance beyond pilot (O'Reilly 2024).
- **Tier 4:** CEO as architect of operating model redesign; active executive sponsors make firms 1.8× more likely to scale (BCG).
- **Three critical mistakes:** (1) Over-investing in Tier 4 before Tier 1/2 foundations; (2) same governance applied to all tiers; (3) ignoring skill creation and sharing.
- **Huble research:** 57% of leaders say they are AI-ready; only 8% actually are.

## European Context

### EU AI Act creates mandatory Tier 1 obligations

- **Article 4 (effective 2 February 2025):** Organisations must promote AI literacy among all staff interacting with AI systems. This creates a regulatory floor for Tier 1 that does not exist in the US or Asia. Provides budget justification for the individual fluency investment that most organisations underweight.
- **High-risk AI systems** (HR recruitment, credit scoring, critical infrastructure): extensive obligations by August 2026 — risk management, human oversight, conformity assessments. Penalties up to €35 million or 7% of global annual turnover.
- **GDPR Article 22:** General prohibition on decisions based solely on automated processing with significant effects. Mandatory safeguards: right to human intervention, right to express one's point of view, right to contest decisions. Directly shapes Tier 3 and Tier 4 deployment; requires human-in-the-loop design for automated decision-making affecting employees or customers.

### Works councils shape pace and process

- **Germany (§87(1) No. 6 BetrVG):** Works councils have co-determination rights over monitoring technology; Hamburg Labour Court (January 2024): AI tools through company accounts trigger co-determination. Siemens, Deutsche Telekom, IBM Germany have negotiated "agile company agreements" on AI.
- **France (Nanterre Court ruling):** Even pilot AI deployment on select employees requires prior works council consultation — stealth pilots are legally impermissible.
- **Nordic countries:** Norway recognised "data shop steward" role in 2022–25 Main Agreement.
- **EU AI Act Article 26(7):** Employers must inform employee representatives "in a clear and comprehensive manner" before deploying high-risk AI systems. Best practice: engage well in advance of any acquisition.

### Regulation as competitive advantage (not constraint)

- Mandatory AI literacy = budget justification and strategic accelerator for Tier 1.
- Works council consultation = builds the trust and psychological safety essential for Tier 2.
- GDPR human-in-the-loop = enforces the governance guardrails that prevent Tier 3/4 failures.
- **Eurostat (2025):** EU AI enterprise adoption reached 20% (from 13.5% in 2024); Denmark 42%, Finland 37.8%, Sweden 35%.
- **Glass Lewis (2025 proxy season):** Only 20.7% of large-cap European companies had formal AI policies; 61.3% had no AI policy disclosed. Nine in ten European investors rejected the notion it is "too early" to hold boards accountable.
- **European companies:** Siemens ("AI with Purpose"), Bosch (measured, augmentation-focused messaging), 13-company European AI Initiative (Airbus, ASML, AXA, BNP Paribas, Deutsche Bank, L'Oréal, Lufthansa, Mercedes-Benz, Orange, Porsche, Siemens, Spotify, Volkswagen).

## Case Study Data — ING (Netherlands)
