# Research Brief — Chapter 11 — Building Your Agentic Organisation

## Framework Evidence

**The Transformation Playbook: Foundation → Activation → Scale — supporting data and expert positions**

- **90% of transformative AI use cases remain stuck in pilot mode** — McKinsey CEO Playbook, June 2025. Root cause: sequencing and scaling failure, not technology failure.
- **95% of enterprise generative AI implementations delivered zero P&L impact** despite $30–40 billion invested — MIT NANDA Report, 2025. Measured at 6 months post-pilot.
- **42% of companies abandoned most AI initiatives in 2025**, up from 17% in 2024 — S&P Global.
- **87–95% of AI pilots never reach production** — multiple sources (GAI Forum Pilot Purgatory Index, Entrepreneur 2025).
- **Organisations underestimate scaling costs by 250–400%** — common finding; 85% miss AI cost projections by over 10%.
- **BCG's 10/20/70 rule**: 10% effort → ML models/algorithms; 20% → data and technology backbone; **70% → people and processes**. Most organisations invert this. — BCG; applies to every phase.
- **BCG's 10/20/70 rule and the 70% wall**: Companies that scale without operating model redesign hit the 70% wall — automation without people/process redesign fails.
- **Organisations with well-structured AI CoEs are 2.6x more likely to successfully scale AI initiatives** — multiple CoE research sources.
- **Companies investing in trust-enabling activities are 2x more likely to see 10%+ revenue growth** — McKinsey.
- **Only 6% of organisations qualify as "AI high performers"** (>5% of EBIT from AI) — McKinsey, 2025.
- **21% of gen AI users report having fundamentally redesigned workflows** — and this is the single most impactful attribute for EBIT impact — McKinsey, 2025.
- **Digital initiatives deliver 15–20% ROI independently; combined with AI reach 30–35% returns** — BCG, 2026. Foundation readiness multiplies Activation returns.
- **BCG's "targets over tools" principle**: Design backward from business outcomes, not forward from technology. Strategy must be tied to growth, cost, and productivity.
- **McKinsey's five-step change management framework**: (1) North Star based on outcomes, (2) build trust with governance, (3) reimagine workflows toward AI teams, (4) identify superusers (5–10% of workforce), (5) continual learning ecosystem.
- **McKinsey's six shifts for the agentic organisation**: Workflows (AI-first end-to-end), Leadership (new capabilities), Talent (redesigned roles), Skilling and Culture (continuous reinvention), Structure (value-creation centred), HR and Talent Systems (engine of AI transformation).
- **BCG's autonomy progression**: Shadow Mode → Supervised Autonomy → Agent-Led with Oversight → Full Autonomy. Organisations expect **250% increase in AI decision-making authority within 3 years** — BCG/MIT Sloan, 2025.
- **McKinsey vision**: "One manager with a small human team could orchestrate hundreds of agents acting autonomously in a workflow cutting across silos, running 24/7."
- **Bain's "micro-productivity trap"**: Proliferation of proofs of concept and isolated use cases delivering modest gains that fail to scale. Grassroots experimentation does not self-organise into enterprise-wide impact without clear direction from the top.
- **BCG's Deploy-Reshape-Invent (DRI) framework**: Deploy (10–15% productivity boost) → Reshape (significant operational transformation) → Invent (new business models). AI-mature companies generate 72% of AI value in core functions (operations, marketing, sales). Most companies stuck at Deploy.
- **Gartner**: Over 40% of agentic AI projects will be cancelled by end of 2027 due to escalating costs, unclear business value, or inadequate risk controls.
- **Deloitte**: Only 21% of organisations have rigorous governance in place even as deployment accelerates (2026).
- **66% of boards report limited-to-no AI knowledge**; only **15% receive AI-related metrics** — McKinsey, book-research-claude.
- **Organisations with active executive sponsorship are 3.3x more likely to report value creation exceeding costs** — CoE research.
- **Hub-and-spoke CoE model**: Deloitte research shows 40% faster implementation timelines vs fully centralised.
- **McKinsey's "domain leader"**: The N-2/N-3 executive who translates vision into operational change is "probably the single most critical role any business needs for its AI transformations."

## European Context

- **EU AI Act timeline**: Prohibited AI practices + AI literacy obligations (Article 4) effective **February 2, 2025**. GPAI transparency requirements **August 2, 2025**. Full high-risk AI system requirements **August 2, 2026** (potentially extended to December 2027 via Digital Omnibus — do not count on it).
- **EU AI Act Article 4**: Providers and deployers must ensure staff possess "sufficient level of AI literacy." Context-dependent; different roles require different competencies. Non-compliance: fines up to €35 million or 7% of global annual turnover.
- **Works councils and social dialogue**: IndustriAll Europe adopted comprehensive trade union strategy on AI at the workplace (November 2024). Works councils have rights to information and consultation on new technologies. European organisations must factor **2–4 months of works council consultation** into Foundation timelines. Treat social dialogue as a governance feature that builds workforce trust, not a cost.
- **Allianz exemplar**: Signed EU AI Pact November 2024 — proactive compliance as competitive advantage. Governance framework across 70+ countries with 16-dimension Data Maturity assessments, Global Business Glossary, Data Catalogue for tracking data lineage.
- **GDPR**: Data used for AI training, monitoring, personalisation must comply with GDPR (purpose limitation, data minimisation, transparency). Digital Omnibus may weaken some protections — track this.
- **PE and mid-market**: 70% of large PE funds now run dedicated AI CoEs with standardised playbooks across portfolio companies. PE-backed companies that systematically build AI capabilities achieve **nearly twice the return on invested capital** — BCG. Budget: 2–5% of revenue; 93% to people/training. Mid-market: 4–8 weeks Foundation, 2–4 months Activation, 3–5 focused use cases.
- **EU enterprise AI adoption**: 19.95% in 2025 (Eurostat), up from 13.5% in 2024. Over 60% of European firms still at earliest stages of AI maturity.

## Case Study Data — BBVA

**Company**: BBVA (Spain) — European bank, 25 countries.

**Dilemma**: How does a 120,000-person European bank build the Agentic Organisation at enterprise scale — moving from cloud migration and data unification (Foundation) through a multidisciplinary AI Factory and embedded "AI wizards" (Activation) to 80% daily AI engagement (Scale) — while maintaining governance, regulatory compliance, and cultural alignment?

**Foundation phase evidence**:
- Completed global cloud migration to a single data and AI platform
- Established governance across regulatory regimes (Spain, Mexico, Turkey, 25 countries)
- Data platform before AI Factory — sequence matters

**Activation phase evidence**:
- **AI Factory**: Founded 2019. 400+ professionals. Local hubs in Spain, Mexico, Turkey.
- **Multidisciplinary teams**: Data science, ML engineering, solution architecture, analytics, product, design, business — plus specialists in digital law, regulation, privacy, security.
- **300 embedded "AI wizards"** deployed into business units (not centralised only).
- **Four horizontal innovation programmes** for knowledge sharing:
  1. Discussion Club
  2. Quick Studies
  3. Innovation Sprints
  4. X Program
- Team configurations vary by deployment context: customer-facing (UX/design), internal systems (engineering), enhancement of other programmes.
- Strategic alliance with OpenAI: ChatGPT Enterprise deployment.

**Scale phase evidence**:
- **ChatGPT Enterprise deployed to all 120,000 employees** across 25 countries
- **3,000+ custom bots** created by employees (bottom-up skill creation)
- **80% daily engagement** with AI tools
- **Employees saving ~3 hours per week** (quantified productivity gain)
- Agentic employee development as mechanism: embedded wizards, innovation programmes, custom bot creation — compounding value at each phase

**Key metrics (all citable)**:
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Employees | 120,000 |
| Countries | 25 |
| AI Factory professionals | 400+ |
| Embedded "AI wizards" | 300 |
| Custom bots created by employees | 3,000+ |
| Daily AI engagement | 80% |
| Time saved per employee | ~3 hours/week |
| Innovation programmes | 4 (Discussion Club, Quick Studies, Innovation Sprints, X Program) |

**Chair quote**: Carlos Torres Vila: "We were pioneers in the digital and mobile transformation, and we are now entering the AI era with even greater ambition."

**Playbook alignment**: BBVA demonstrates the sequence matters — data platform before AI Factory, AI Factory before enterprise deployment, skill-sharing mechanisms at every stage. Agentic employee development (embedded wizards, innovation programmes, custom bot creation) is the mechanism through which each phase delivers compounding value.

## Foundation Phase — Dense Evidence

**Governance**:
- AI inventory with risk classification mapped to EU AI Act four tiers (unacceptable, high-risk, limited, minimal)
- Chief AI Officer or equivalent with cross-functional mandate
- Tiered autonomy policies, comprehensive logging, circuit breakers, pre-deployment assessment
- Shadow AI policy (employees deploying unapproved tools)
- Board reporting cadence — only 15% of boards currently receive AI metrics
- Allianz: 16-dimension Data Maturity assessments, Global Business Glossary, Data Catalogue

**Data readiness — 120-day sprint** (CIO.com, 2026):
| Days | Focus | Key actions |
|------|-------|-------------|
| 0–30 | Unified AI/data foundation | Data audit, governance ownership model, architecture health check |
| 30–60 | Governance and policy controls | Cross-functional data governance council (IT, legal, finance, operations), lineage and cataloguing standards, quality gates |
| 60–90 | Interoperable foundations | Semantic data models, connect disparate sources, reusable data products |
| 90–120 | Secure AI operationalisation | First data pipelines for AI workloads, governance tested, regulatory compliance validated |

- **Fewer than 1 in 5 organisations** have high data maturity (WEF)
- **57% estimate their data is not AI-ready** (Gartner); only 12% globally have truly AI-ready data
- McKinsey: "Walled data gardens" — proprietary data as differentiator when foundation models commoditise

**AI literacy**:
- EU AI Act Article 4: literacy obligations already in force (Feb 2025)
- Five dimensions: basic knowledge, prompting/interaction, validation/judgment, ethics/security, attitude/openness
- **"Have you asked the AI?"** — McKinsey Lilli: 92% staff usage, 74% regular usage, 30%+ time saved on information gathering. Cultural mechanism: leadership asking in every meeting.
- Foundational training on three areas (when to use, how to use, what AI is) **increases AI usage 3x** — Go1, 2026
- Mandatory, not voluntary. BCG "Trailblazer" CEOs: 6+ hours weekly upskilling, 3x investment, 2x workforce upskilling
- 70% use AI weekly; only 56% feel confident selecting the right tool (Go1, 2026)

**Agentic employee at Foundation**:
- Embedded learning in daily workflows (not classroom events)
- Skill-sharing incentives: recognition, dedicated time, platforms for prompt templates and workflow automations
- Baseline fluency goals per role family
- Shopify: AI usage baseline expectation; teams must demonstrate why AI cannot do a job before requesting headcount

**Duration**: 4–6 months (large enterprise); 2–4 months (mid-market); 4–8 weeks (PE-backed)

## Activation Phase — Dense Evidence

**Strategy setting with AI Transformation Framework** (four layers):
- Vision: What does our AI-enabled future look like?
- Products: Which customer journeys do we transform first?
- Operating Model: How do we staff blended teams?
- Foundation: Is our infrastructure ready?

**Centre of Excellence**:
- Start centralised → transition to hub-and-spoke as maturity grows
- Core team: AI/ML Engineers, Data Scientists, AI Product Managers, AI Governance Specialists, Business Translators
- Charter: executive sponsorship, 3–5 priority use cases, success metrics, mandate to demonstrate measurable outcomes within **60–90 days**
- BBVA: 400+ professionals, 300 embedded wizards, four innovation programmes

**Pilots — selection and gating**:
- Use AI Decision Framework (Value, Capabilities, Trust) to score every pilot before launch
- Success criteria before launch: resolution rates, time saved, error reduction, P&L impact
- Human-in-the-loop checkpoints in every pilot
- **Pilot-to-production gating checklist**: business case validated with actual data; scaling cost estimated (250–400% underestimation typical); data pipeline production-ready; governance verified; change management plan; template for replication
- Allianz Project Nemo: 7 specialised agents, **80% reduction in claims processing time**, built in **under 100 days**, human-in-the-loop for all payout decisions

**First wins and storytelling**:
- Choose structured, repeatable processes with clear before/after metrics
- Narrate: problem → solution → quantified impact → lessons → next steps
- Tell the story to the board — vehicle for moving from 15% to 100% on AI board reporting
- Template first win for replication

**Blended human–agent teams**:
- Agents as team members (not tools); transparent identity; clear handoffs; psychological safety
- "Two-in-the-box": business leader and technology leader co-own each initiative
- Superusers: 5–10% of workforce as internal champions
- Five strategic human roles (Sherbaum, 2026): Trainer, Fallback, Reviewer, Verifier, Orchestrator

**Agentic employee at Activation**: Shift from individual fluency to team coordination. First supervisor, specialist, augmented frontline roles emerge. Employees move from consuming skills to creating and sharing them.

**Duration**: 6–12 months (large enterprise); 3–6 months (mid-market); 2–4 months (PE-backed)

## Scale Phase — Dense Evidence

**Cross-functional transformation**:
- Enterprise North Star transcending departmental boundaries
- Cross-functional task forces own end-to-end workflows
- Budget and talent reallocation from siloed initiatives to enterprise programmes
- AI oversight committee with enterprise-wide mandate
- **What breaks**: politics (department heads resist), misaligned incentives, no agreed enterprise KPIs, change saturation (75% at or past; 1 in 3 sabotage rollouts)

**Operating model redesign — McKinsey's six shifts**:
1. Workflows — AI-first end-to-end
2. Leadership — new capabilities
3. Talent — redesigned roles and profiles
4. Skilling and Culture — continuous reinvention
5. Structure — value-creation centred
6. HR and Talent Systems — engine of AI transformation

**Metrics at Scale** (four categories):
- **Financial**: cost savings, revenue impact, % EBIT from AI
- **Operational**: efficiency, error reduction, cycle time
- **Adoption**: usage rates, % workflows redesigned, fluency levels
- **Strategic**: CSAT/NPS, decision quality, innovation velocity

**Agentic employee at Scale**: All three profiles (supervisor, specialist, augmented frontline) are the norm. Skill-sharing is organisational capability with formal skill libraries and peer training. New roles: AI architects, deployment strategists, AI conversation designers. Compounding effect: one person's innovation multiplies across the organisation.

**Duration**: 12–24 months (large enterprise); 6–12 months (mid-market and PE-backed)

## Phase Gates and Board Accountability

**"Foundation Complete" gate**: AI governance framework operational; data foundation sufficient for priority use cases; baseline AI literacy achieved and measured; EU AI Act compliance met; works council/social dialogue initiated; CoE established with charter and executive sponsor.

**"Activation Complete" gate**: 2–3 pilots moved to production with measurable P&L impact; CoE at hub-and-spoke level; blended human–agent teams operational in at least one department; governance scaled to production; first wins documented; board receiving regular AI metrics.

**"Scale Ready" gate**: Cross-functional workflows in production; operating model redesign underway; workforce-level AI fluency programme in execution; enterprise KPIs defined and tracked; governance mature for increased agent autonomy.

**CEO role by phase**: Foundation — set mandate, model AI usage (4–6 hours/week); Activation — champion pilots, remove barriers, tell first-win story to board; Scale — drive operating model redesign, set enterprise North Star.

## Sequencing Failure Patterns (Beat 2)

- **(a) Launching pilots without governance** — scrambling when EU AI Act deadlines hit
- **(b) Building CoE without data readiness** — CoE produces proofs of concept that cannot move to production
- **(c) Declaring "Scale" without operating model redesign** — micro-productivity trap (Bain); automating existing workflows rather than redesigning
- **(d) Ignoring the people track** — only 6% have begun meaningful upskilling (BCG) despite 89% acknowledging need; 75% at or past change saturation; 1 in 3 workers actively sabotage AI rollouts

## Agentic Employee — Three Profiles (from the-agentic-employee.md)

| Profile | Role | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Supervisor** | Orchestrates multiple AI agents across workflows | Project manager coordinating AI agents for scheduling, reporting, resource allocation |
| **Specialist** | Handles exceptions and edge cases agents cannot resolve | Compliance officer reviewing flagged transactions agents escalated |
| **Augmented Frontline Worker** | Uses AI to eliminate repetitive tasks, focus on human-centric work | Customer success rep whose agent handles data gathering |

**Mindset shift**: "I do the work" → "I orchestrate the work"; "AI is a tool" → "AI agents are teammates"; "My value is what I produce" → "My value is the outcomes I enable"; "I learn skills" → "I create and share skills to multiply impact."
