### Escalation: BBVA Duplicate Case Study

BBVA has been the primary case study in three chapters:
- **Chapter 5:** "BBVA, Deploying One AI Transformation Map Across 120,000 Employees" (framework adoption across 120,000 employees)
- **Chapter 11:** "BBVA — Building the Agentic Organisation at Enterprise Scale" (sequencing and playbook execution)
- **Chapter 14:** "BBVA: The Decision to Stop Piloting" (leadership decision and pilot-to-production transition)

Each use covers a distinct lens (framework adoption, sequencing, leadership decision), but the criterion is not met regardless of the angle: the same company is the primary case study three times.

The Orchestrator should decide between two options:

**(a) Accept the third BBVA use given its distinct angle and the chapter's nature as a conclusion.** The case study explicitly uses BBVA to illustrate a principle that spans all previous chapters (the leadership decision to stop piloting), and each use covers genuinely different material. A conclusion chapter summarising the journey of the book's lead case company could be argued as structurally justified. If accepted, no changes are needed to the case study in ch14-final.md other than the paragraph-length and em-dash fixes already applied.

**(b) Commission a replacement case study.** EQT is mentioned in the body text as "proving that agentic transformation works in PE-backed mid-market companies across Europe" and has not been used as a primary case study in any chapter. EQT could illustrate the same leadership-decision principle from a PE/mid-market angle, which would also broaden the book's coverage and close a gap (no PE-backed primary case study in the book). Schneider Electric (Chapter 9 primary case) and ING (Chapter 4 primary case) are already used and should not be repeated. Siemens (Chapter 1 primary case) is also already used. EQT is the strongest candidate.
