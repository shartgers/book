##About the author

Stephan Hartgers-Rus is a former McKinsey strategy consultant with more than 25 years of experience helping organizations create real value from technology, long before AI became a boardroom imperative. Before becoming a consultant, Stephan studied Econometrics and Computer Science, fields that are especially relevant to the rise of AI today. 

During his tenure at McKinsey, Stephan led high-stakes transformation programs before moving to senior leadership roles at digital agency Mobiquity, where he served clients worldwide. Later, he spearheaded the development of several company-wide transformation programs and ways of working for both IT innovation (establishing the foundational digital innovation framework) and, later, the AI-native product development cycle. This makes him uniquely positioned to guide organisations through the next wave of AI-driven change.

Today, through his independent advisory practice, Stephan works with CEOs, CIOs, and leadership teams navigating the transition to an AI-shaped world. His premise is simple: AI is no longer a technology question. It is a fundamental leadership question. And most organizations are not yet asking the right ones.

The Agentic Organisation is the distillation of those years in the field, the patterns he has seen, the failures he has witnessed, and the handful of organizations that got it right. It is written for leaders who want strategic clarity, not hype, and who understand that the real work of AI transformation happens in the organization, not the algorithm.

Stephan is married, has two beautiful daughters, and lives in The Hague. When he is not advising companies, you will likely find him on the water, kite-surfing.
