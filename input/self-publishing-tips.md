# Self Publishing Playbook  
Operational guidance for inclusion in CLAUDE.md

This document provides structured instructions for an AI agent supporting the self publication of a serious managerial book aimed at executives, supervisory boards, private equity investors and senior leadership teams.

The objective is intellectual authority, production quality and commercial credibility.

---

# 1. Strategic Positioning Before Writing

Self publishing success begins before the manuscript is complete.

## 1.1 Define Market Position

The AI must help clarify:

- Primary audience: board members, CEOs, PE partners, supervisory boards
- Core promise: the central leadership problem the book addresses
- Category: strategy, governance, transformation, AI and leadership
- Differentiation: how the book stands apart from mainstream business titles

AI Instructions:
- Formulate a precise one sentence positioning statement.
- Draft back cover promise early in the writing process.
- Ensure each chapter reinforces the positioning.

---

# 2. Publishing Platforms

## 2.1 Amazon KDP

Entity: Amazon KDP  
Type: Company  
Description: Self publishing platform owned by Amazon

Strengths:
- Global distribution
- Print on demand
- Ebook and paperback
- Fast time to market
- Attractive royalties

Limitations:
- Amazon centric ecosystem
- Perceived as less prestigious in certain executive circles

Use case:
- Broad reach and market validation.

---

## 2.2 IngramSpark

Entity: IngramSpark  
Type: Company  
Description: Professional self publishing and distribution platform

Strengths:
- Bookstore distribution
- Hardcover options
- International reach

Limitations:
- More complex setup
- Upfront costs

Use case:
- Executive credibility
- Corporate bulk orders
- Hardback editions

---

## 2.3 Recommended Approach

For a serious managerial book:

- Use Amazon KDP for ebook and paperback.
- Use IngramSpark for hardcover and bookstore distribution.
- Consider direct sales via personal website.

AI Instruction:
- Design metadata and descriptions suitable for both platforms.

---

# 3. Professional Production Standards

## 3.1 Editing Phases

Mandatory stages:

1. Developmental editing  
   Structure, argument, positioning

2. Line editing  
   Tone, clarity, authority

3. Proofreading  
   Final corrections and consistency

AI Instruction:
- Deliver structurally coherent chapters.
- Avoid repetition and verbosity.
- Assume a human editor refines but does not reconstruct.

---

## 3.2 Cover Design

Requirements:

- Strong title hierarchy
- Clear and credible subtitle
- Author name positioned professionally
- Elegant typography
- High contrast design

Avoid:

- Generic business imagery
- Overused symbols such as light bulbs or abstract brains

AI Instruction:
- Generate multiple subtitle options.
- Draft concise back cover copy.
- Propose positioning for endorsements.

---

## 3.3 Interior Layout

Recommended tools:

- Adobe InDesign
- Vellum
- Atticus

Principles:

- Generous margins
- Clear heading hierarchy
- Consistent chapter openings
- Balanced white space

For executive audiences:

- Use diagrams sparingly
- Prioritize readability over density

---

# 4. ISBN and Legal Structure

## 4.1 ISBN Ownership

Purchase independent ISBNs.

Netherlands registration authority: Bureau ISBN

Benefits:

- Professional credibility
- Control across formats
- Independence from platform lock in

Acquire separate ISBNs for:

- Ebook
- Paperback
- Hardcover

---

## 4.2 Imprint Strategy

Consider creating a publishing imprint name.

Benefits:

- Professional perception
- Separation of advisory brand and publishing entity
- Future scalability

---

# 5. Metadata and Discoverability

## 5.1 Categories

- Select narrow subcategories.
- Aim for visibility within defined niches.
- Avoid overly broad categories.

## 5.2 Keywords

Develop clusters around:

- AI governance
- Board strategy
- Digital transformation leadership
- Private equity transformation
- Executive decision making

AI Instruction:
- Generate keyword clusters.
- Align subtitle with search intent.
- Avoid vague terminology.

---

# 6. Pricing Strategy

Recommended pricing ranges:

- Ebook: 9.99 to 14.99 EUR
- Paperback: 19.99 to 29.99 EUR
- Hardcover: 29.99 to 39.99 EUR

For executive audiences:

- Premium pricing supports authority.
- Avoid mass market discount positioning.

---

# 7. Launch Strategy

## 7.1 Pre Launch Phase

Timeline: 3 to 6 months before publication

Actions:

- Build targeted mailing list
- Publish thought leadership content
- Appear on podcasts
- Test excerpts with trusted executives

AI Instruction:
- Draft a 90 day launch calendar.
- Create LinkedIn content derived from chapters.
- Draft outreach templates for executive peers.

---

## 7.2 Advance Review Copies

Distribute to:

- CEOs
- Supervisory board members
- PE partners
- Recognized industry experts

Objective:

- Secure endorsements
- Generate early reviews

---

## 7.3 Launch Week Execution

- Coordinated LinkedIn presence
- Direct email campaign
- Personal outreach
- Encourage verified reviews

Early momentum influences platform algorithms.

---

# 8. Reviews and Social Proof

Target:

- 20 to 50 reviews in first month
- High credibility endorsements

Avoid:

- Artificial review manipulation
- Incentivized schemes

For executive books:

- Quality of endorsement outweighs volume.

---

# 9. Corporate and Bulk Sales Strategy

For a managerial book, bulk sales often exceed retail sales in strategic value.

Approach:

- Offer customized foreword for corporate clients
- Bundle book with board workshops
- Create executive briefing formats linked to the book

AI Instruction:
- Draft corporate sales one pager.
- Draft outreach email for PE firms.
- Design workshop concepts aligned with book themes.

---

# 10. Long Term Positioning

The book should function as:

- Authority signal
- Strategic conversation starter
- Foundation for speaking engagements
- Entry point to advisory mandates

AI Instruction:
- Ensure the manuscript strengthens intellectual credibility.
- Reinforce independence and thoughtfulness.
- Avoid hype language.
- Focus on governance, risk, decision making and capital allocation.

---

# 11. Timeline Overview

Indicative schedule:

- Writing: 4 to 8 months
- Editing: 1 to 2 months
- Design and formatting: 1 month
- Pre launch build: overlaps final 3 months
- Launch window: 4 weeks
- Post launch leverage: ongoing

Total expected timeline: 6 to 12 months.

---

# 12. Quality Standard

The book must feel:

- Calm and authoritative
- Strategically grounded
- Independent
- Suitable for board level discussion

The AI must avoid:

- Hype driven language
- Startup clichés
- Over simplification

The book must provide:

- Structured reasoning
- Executive level examples
- Clear frameworks
- Practical strategic reflection
