# Deep Research Prompt: Chapter 7 — What the Leaders Did

## Purpose of This Prompt

Use this prompt to conduct **extensive external research** on the six case-study companies (BBVA, ING, Siemens, Allianz, Klarna, Schneider Electric) and on other European or global leaders, to deepen dilemmas, decisions, and transferable lessons. The goal is to gather material for the **research** folder to support writing this long, case-study-heavy chapter.

---

## Chapter Context (What We Already Know)

**Chapter title:** What the Leaders Did  
**Place in book:** Part III — The European Advantage  
**Target length:** ~15% of book (~5,250 words) — longest chapter; six case studies  
**Core argument:** Deep case studies: the dilemmas leaders faced, the decisions they took, and the transferable lessons for the reader.

### Evidence already in our research (summary)

- **BBVA (Spain):** ChatGPT Enterprise to 120,000 employees, 25 countries; 80% daily engagement, ~3 hours/week saved; 3,000+ custom bots; AI Factory 400+, 300 embedded “AI wizards.” Carlos Torres Vila: “pioneers in digital and mobile … entering the AI era with even greater ambition.”
- **ING (Netherlands):** 500 analytics, 90% pilot-to-production (vs ~30% industry); 75% customer queries automated; 25% productivity gains (COO); Yilmaz on domain knowledge and “where it matters to the client.”
- **Siemens (Germany):** Hermes Award 2025 Industrial Copilot; Nanjing Digital Native Factory (200% capacity, 20% productivity); Amberg 20% downtime reduction; NVIDIA “Industrial AI Operating System”; target up to 50% productivity for industrial companies. Cedrik Neike on multimodal industrial-grade models.
- **Allianz (Germany):** #2 Evident AI Insurance Index; ~10% of AI workforce across 30 major insurers; ~400 GenAI use cases; Project Nemo: 80% claim processing time reduction, 7-agent workflow <5 min. Barbara Karuth-Zelle: “mindset, people, organisation” over technology. EU AI Pact Nov 2024.
- **Klarna (Sweden):** AI assistant 2.3M conversations/month (≈700 agents); resolution 11 min → <2 min; $40M profit improvement; later re-hired humans for complex/emotional cases. Siemiatkowski: “AI gives us speed. Talent gives us empathy.” 86% prefer empathy over speed for complex issues.
- **Schneider Electric (France):** 6-day inventory reduction, 15% yield improvement, >€100M value; Philippe Rambach, Chief AI Officer; AI Hub; target €2.0–2.5B industrial productivity (2026–2030).
- **Other references in research:** DBS (Singapore), Shopify, GitHub Copilot, Kore.ai wealth management — for comparison or supporting points.

### Book thesis

This chapter is the main evidence base for “what the leaders did”: each case should have a clear dilemma, decision, outcome, and lesson so a European CEO can see themselves and transfer the lesson.

## Main Concepts to Research Deeply (External Focus)

1. **BBVA**
   - Chronology: when and how the OpenAI alliance and ChatGPT Enterprise rollout were decided; board and CEO role; how “AI Factory” and “AI wizards” were designed and embedded.
   - Dilemma: e.g. scale vs control, speed vs governance, build vs partner; any public framing of the strategic choice.
   - Metrics: source and timing of 80% engagement, 3 hours/week, 3,000 bots; any updates (2025–2026); revenue or cost impact if disclosed.
   - Lessons and quotes: Torres Vila and other executives on ambition, digital legacy, and AI; any setbacks or pivots.
   - European angle: Spanish/European regulation, workforce, market context.

2. **ING**
   - How 90% pilot-to-production was achieved (governance, criteria, culture); comparison to industry 30%; role of 500-strong analytics team and COO.
   - Dilemma: e.g. central vs decentralised AI; prioritisation; “where it matters to the client” as filter.
   - Yilmaz and other leaders: more quotes on pragmatism, domain knowledge, and European approach.
   - Metrics: 75% queries automated, 25% productivity; sources and updates; any other disclosed KPIs.
   - Netherlands/ EU context: regulation, talent, ecosystem (Eindhoven, etc.).

3. **Siemens**
   - Industrial Copilot and Hermes Award: what it does, how it was developed, role of partners (e.g. NVIDIA).
   - Nanjing and Amberg: detailed outcomes (200%, 20%, 20% downtime); timeline and context.
   - Dilemma: e.g. industrial safety vs speed; global vs local deployment; build vs partner for “industrial AI OS.”
   - Cedrik Neike and Roland Busch: quotes on industrial AI, multimodal models, and strategy.
   - Germany and DACH: Industry 4.0, Mittelstand, regulatory environment.

4. **Allianz**
   - Project Nemo: 7-agent workflow, <5 min, 80% time reduction; design, ownership, and rollout.
   - Evident AI Insurance Index: what it measures; why Allianz ranks #2; comparison to peers.
   - ~400 GenAI use cases: how they are governed, prioritised, and scaled; role of central vs business units.
   - EU AI Pact: what Allianz committed to; how it is used in narrative (trust, compliance).
   - Barbara Karuth-Zelle: “mindset, people, organisation”; other leadership quotes; HR and culture initiatives.
   - Insurance-specific: regulation, claims, underwriting, customer experience.

5. **Klarna**
   - Full arc: launch of AI assistant, 2.3M conversations, metrics, then partial reversal (re-hiring for complex/emotional).
   - Siemiatkowski’s public statements: “speed vs empathy”; 86% customer preference; lesson that hybrid is the endgame.
   - Dilemma: scale vs quality; cost vs experience; when to bring humans back in.
   - Updates (2025–2026): any further evolution of the hybrid model or new metrics.
   - Sweden / Nordic context: labour, regulation, market.

6. **Schneider Electric**
   - Philippe Rambach and AI Hub: structure, mandate, and link to business units.
   - Value: 6-day inventory, 15% yield, €100M+; methodology and time period; €2.0–2.5B target (2026–2030) — breakdown by domain if available.
   - Dilemma: e.g. industrial reliability vs AI adoption; global footprint and local deployment.
   - France and EU: national AI strategy, regulation, energy/industrial policy.

7. **Transferable lessons and additional cases**
   - Cross-case themes: CEO ownership, governance, pilot-to-production, hybrid human–AI, trust and compliance, metrics that matter.
   - One or two additional European cases (if strong dilemma–decision–lesson): e.g. other banks, insurers, industrials, or PE-backed firms.
   - Global benchmarks (DBS, Shopify, JPMorgan) used only to reinforce lessons, not as main cases — any new data or quotes.

## Suggested Research Questions

- For each of the six companies: what was the single biggest strategic dilemma they have articulated (or can be inferred), and what was the decisive decision?
- What exact metrics and timeframes are publicly available for each case, and what is the primary source (annual report, press, analyst, conference)?
- Have any of the six published setbacks, reversals, or “lessons learned” (e.g. Klarna’s hybrid pivot) that we can use for nuance?
- Are there CEO or C-suite interviews (2024–2026) that add new quotes or lessons for each company?
- What European companies in the same sectors (banking, insurance, industrial, tech) have comparable public case studies we could use as backup or contrast?
- How do we frame “transferable lesson” so it is actionable for a CEO in a different sector or size?
