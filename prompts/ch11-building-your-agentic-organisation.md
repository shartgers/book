# Deep Research Prompt: Chapter 11 — Building Your Agentic Organisation

## Purpose of This Prompt

Use this prompt to conduct **extensive external research** on the phased playbook (Foundation → Activation → Scale) and on developing agentic employees at each phase. The goal is to gather frameworks, examples, and practical guidance for the **research** folder to support writing this **playbook** chapter.

## Chapter Context (What We Already Know)

**Chapter title:** Building Your Agentic Organisation  
**Place in book:** Part IV — The CEO's Playbook  
**Target length:** ~9% of book (~4,050 words)  
**Core argument:** A phased approach: **Foundation** (governance, data readiness, AI literacy) → **Activation** (strategy setting with the four layers of the AI Transformation Framework—Vision, Products, Operating Model, Foundation—Centre of Excellence, pilots, first wins) → **Scale** (cross-functional transformation, operating model redesign). Practical guidance on developing agentic employees at every phase: embedded learning and skill-sharing in Foundation; blended human–agent teams in Activation; full workforce-level agentic capability at Scale.

### Evidence already in our research

- **Foundation:** Governance (EU AI Act, risk classification, policies); data readiness (unified foundation, governance, 120-day roadmap); AI literacy (training, "Have you asked the AI?"); agentic employees: embedded learning, skill-sharing incentives, baseline fluency.
- **Activation:** CoE (centralised then hub-and-spoke; demonstrate value in 60–90 days); pilots with clear success criteria; first wins and storytelling; blended human–agent teams; superusers and "two-in-the-box."
- **Scale:** Cross-functional transformation; operating model redesign; agentic networks; full workforce agentic capability; trust-enabling activities and 2× likelihood of 10%+ revenue growth (McKinsey).
- **Agentic employee development:** Research/the-agentic-employee.md: supervisor, specialist, augmented frontline; skill creation and sharing; compounding effect. McKinsey: experimenters → accelerators; North Star, trust, reimagine workflows, superusers, continual learning.
- **Related:** Four tiers (Ch 4), Value/Capability/Trust (Ch 8), readiness and five pillars (Ch 12), KPIs (Ch 13); CoE models, pilot purgatory, 90% stuck in pilot (McKinsey).

### Book thesis

This chapter is the main "how to build it" playbook. It must be actionable: phases, sequence, and what to do for people (agentic employees) at each phase, so a European CEO can draft a roadmap.

## Main Concepts to Research Deeply (External Focus)

1. **Foundation phase: governance, data, literacy**
   - **Governance:** What "AI governance" means in the Foundation phase (policies, risk classification, ownership, board reporting); EU AI Act minimum viable compliance; how companies that started early structured this.
   - **Data readiness:** 120-day or similar roadmaps; unified data foundation; data quality and governance for AI; "walled gardens"; practical steps and typical timelines; failures due to skipping data foundation.
   - **AI literacy:** What "literacy" includes (prompting, validation, ethics, security); mandatory vs voluntary; how leading companies measure literacy; "Have you asked Lilli?" type norms; role of leadership in modelling.
   - **Agentic employee at Foundation:** Embedded learning (where and how); skill-sharing incentives (recognition, time, platforms); baseline fluency goals; how to avoid "optional" adoption.
   - **European specifics:** Works council and legal constraints on training/monitoring; GDPR and data use; EU AI Act training obligations.

2. **Activation phase: strategy setting with AI Transformation Framework, CoE, pilots, first wins**
   - **Strategy setting with the AI Transformation Framework (four layers: Vision, Products, Operating Model, Foundation):** How organisations set and align strategy across the four framework layers at the start of Activation; linking Foundation readiness to strategic priorities; practical approaches and common mistakes.
   - **Centre of Excellence:** Centralised vs hub-and-spoke vs embedded; when to transition; composition (business + tech); "demonstrate value in 60–90 days" — what that means in practice; failure modes (CoE as bottleneck, lack of business ownership).
   - **Pilots:** Selection criteria (Value/Capability/Trust; four tiers); success criteria and metrics; how to avoid pilot purgatory (McKinsey: 90% stuck); gating: pilot → production decision process.
   - **First wins:** Choosing early use cases for impact and narrative; storytelling internally and to the board; scaling the first win (replication, templates).
   - **Blended human–agent teams:** Design principles; superusers and champions; "two-in-the-box"; handoffs and escalation; psychological safety and experimentation.
   - **Agentic employee at Activation:** Moving from individual fluency to team coordination; creating and sharing skills; first "orchestrator" and "specialist" roles in practice.
   - **Case material:** BBVA, ING, Allianz, Siemens — how they describe their Foundation and Activation (CoE, first pilots, first wins).

3. **Scale phase: cross-functional, operating model**
   - **Cross-functional transformation:** Breaking silos; cross-functional task forces; enterprise North Star; reallocation of budget and talent; what typically breaks (politics, incentives, measurement).
   - **Operating model redesign:** From function-centric to workflow- and outcome-centric; agentic networks; "one manager + small team + hundreds of agents"; accountability and performance management in scaled model.
   - **Scale pitfalls:** Over-centralisation; under-investment in capability (70% people/process); trust and adoption at scale; change saturation (75% at or past); how leading companies avoid these.
   - **Agentic employee at Scale:** Workforce-level agentic capability; supervisor/specialist/augmented frontline at scale; skill-sharing as organisational capability; continuous learning and adaptation.
   - **Metrics at Scale:** Enterprise KPIs (revenue, cost, risk, culture); 2× likelihood of 10%+ revenue with trust (McKinsey); board and ExCo reporting.

4. **Phasing and sequencing**
   - Can Foundation and Activation overlap? What must be in place before Activation (e.g. minimum governance, data, literacy)?
   - Typical duration of each phase (by company size, sector); when to declare "Foundation complete" or "Activation complete."
   - Sequence by four tiers: does Foundation align with Individual/Tier 1, Activation with Team/Department, Scale with Organisation? Or different mapping.
   - European context: regulatory deadlines (e.g. EU AI Act 2026) as forcing function for Foundation; labour and social dialogue as part of phasing.

5. **Practical playbook elements**
   - Checklists or scorecards for "Foundation ready," "Activation ready," "Scale ready."
   - Role of CEO and C-suite in each phase (time, decisions, communications).
   - Templates: pilot proposal, business case (Value/Capability/Trust), CoE charter, literacy curriculum outline.
   - Links to Ch 12 (readiness diagnostic, five pillars) and Ch 13 (KPIs); avoid duplication but ensure consistency.
   - PE and mid-market: shorter phases, lighter CoE, different sequencing?

## Suggested Research Questions

- What do consultancies (McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte, Accenture) recommend as the sequence and content of Foundation / Activation / Scale (or equivalent phases)?
- How do companies that have reached "Scale" describe what they did in Foundation and Activation in retrospect?
- What are the most common reasons organisations get stuck between Activation and Scale, and what do successful scalers do differently?
- How do you measure "AI literacy" and "agentic employee" readiness at Foundation and Activation in practice?
- What does a "Centre of Excellence" charter or mandate look like in practice (scope, governance, resourcing, success metrics)?
- Are there European playbooks or sector playbooks (e.g. banking, insurance, manufacturing) that we can align with or contrast?
