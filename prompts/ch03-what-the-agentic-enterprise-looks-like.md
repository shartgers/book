# Deep Research Prompt: Chapter 3 — What the Agentic Organisation Looks Like

## Purpose of This Prompt

Use this prompt to conduct **extensive external research** on the definition, structure, and human dimension of the Agentic Enterprise. The goal is to gather evidence, frameworks, and examples that can be added to the **research** folder to support writing this chapter (the **model chapter** of the book).

---

## Chapter Context (What We Already Know)

**Chapter title:** What the Agentic Organisation Looks Like  
**Place in book:** Part II — The Model  
**Target length:** ~13% of book (~4,550 words) — densest model chapter  
**Core argument:** A working definition of the Agentic Enterprise and its five elements (Products, People, Process, Technology, Data); the **agentic employee** (human half); three profiles (supervisor, specialist, augmented frontline); mindset shift from task-doer to orchestrator; organisations must redesign roles, not just adopt tools.

### Evidence already in our research

- **McKinsey “Agentic Organization” (Sept 2025):** “Largest organizational paradigm shift since the industrial and digital revolutions”; organisation that “unites humans and AI agents at scale at near-zero marginal cost”; five pillars: Business Model, Operating Model, Governance, Workforce/People/Culture, Technology/Data. Vision: “One manager with a small human team could orchestrate hundreds of agents … cutting across silos, running 24/7.”
- **BCG:** Agentic AI as “frontal cortex” (executive function); 10/20/70 rule (algorithms 10%, tech 20%, people/process 70%); 76% of executives see agentic AI as “coworker”; 35% of companies use agentic AI; 95% in extensive-adoption firms report improved job satisfaction; 250% increase in AI decision-making authority expected in 3 years. Autonomy tiers: Shadow → supervised → agent-led with oversight → full autonomy.
- **Deloitte:** “Operating logic of the future”; “agentification”; 25% → 50% of GenAI users launching agentic pilots by 2025/2027; 80% of agent deployment work is “unglamorous” (data, governance, workflow).
- **Gartner/Forrester:** Gartner: 40%+ agentic projects cancelled by end 2027; “agent washing.” Forrester: Agent as Skill vs Agent as Product; Skills Roadmap vs Foundations Roadmap.
- **Five elements (our book):** Products, People, Process, Technology, Data — with rich sub-points in research (AI-native products, MCP/A2A, data readiness, etc.).
- **Agentic employee (research/the-agentic-employee.md):** Works alongside autonomous agents as teammates; orchestrates; creates reusable AI workflows; judgment at the right moments. Three profiles: **supervisor** (orchestrate multiple agents), **specialist** (exceptions/escalations), **augmented frontline** (freed from repetitive work). Compounding effect: creating and sharing skills across the organisation.
- **Tool vs organisation:** Using agents as tools = optimise existing processes; building Agentic Enterprise = redesign decisions, work flow, human–machine collaboration.

### Book thesis

European CEOs need a clear, actionable model of what they are building. This chapter is the main place we define the Agentic Enterprise and the agentic employee so that later chapters (tiers, value/capability/trust, playbook) can refer back to it.

## Main Concepts to Research Deeply (External Focus)

1. **Definitions and variants of “agentic organisation” / “agentic enterprise”**
   - Other consultancies, vendors, or academics (2024–2026) defining agentic organisation, agentic enterprise, or human–agent collaboration at scale.
   - Distinctions: agentic AI (technology) vs agentic organisation (operating model); any standard or taxonomy (e.g. ISO, NIST, industry bodies).
   - European-specific definitions or frameworks (EU, national agencies, industry associations).

2. **The five elements in practice**
   - **Products:** AI-native products and dynamic customer journeys; “zero-click” discovery; B2B personalisation at scale; European examples.
   - **People:** Role redesign (not just upskilling); new titles (Agent Orchestrator, AI Product Manager, MLOps); domain leader (N-2/N-3) as critical role; 6% “meaningful” upskilling vs 89% acknowledging need.
   - **Process:** Zero-based process design; workflow reimagination; stand-alone agents → agent groups → swarms; MVOs (Minimum Viable Organisations).
   - **Technology:** MCP, A2A, seven-layer stack; CoE vs federated vs embedded; “agentic factory” / “agentic mesh” (McKinsey).
   - **Data:** Data readiness, walled gardens, 120-day roadmaps; Europe-specific data governance (GDPR, EU AI Act data requirements).

3. **The agentic employee (human half of the equation)**
   - Latest research on “human in the loop,” “human–agent teaming,” “orchestrator” roles (academic, consultancy, vendor).
   - Supervisor / specialist / augmented frontline — other frameworks that name or map similar profiles; skill-building and “skill sharing” as organisational capability.
   - Resistance, trust, and job satisfaction: 95% job satisfaction in high agentic adoption (BCG); 1 in 3 sabotage (elsewhere); how to reconcile and what explains the difference (leadership, design, involvement).
   - European workforce and labour relations: works councils, training obligations, “right to disconnect,” and how they affect design of agentic roles.

4. **Mindset shift: task-doer → orchestrator**
   - “Orchestrator” as a competency; how it is defined and measured; training and hiring for it.
   - “Have you asked Lilli?” / “Have you asked the AI?” as cultural norms; diffusion of AI fluency from individuals to teams.
   - Redesigning roles vs “adding tools”: case studies or frameworks that show role redesign (job architecture, RACI, performance criteria) rather than tool rollout alone.

5. **Organisation design and operating model**
   - Org charts “pivoting toward agentic networks”; ratio of humans to agents; manager span (e.g. one manager + small team + hundreds of agents).
   - How accountability, performance management, and incentives change when work is human–agent; hybrid teams and escalation paths.
   - European examples: BBVA, ING, Siemens, Allianz, Schneider — how they describe their operating model and role design, not just use cases.

## Suggested Research Questions

- Beyond McKinsey/BCG/Deloitte, who else has published a formal “agentic organisation” or “agentic enterprise” definition or framework in 2024–2026?
- What concrete job descriptions or role definitions exist for “agent orchestrator,” “AI product manager,” or “supervisor of agents”?
- Is there longitudinal data on job satisfaction, productivity, or retention in organisations that have moved to agentic models vs those that only added tools?
- How do European labour and employment regulations (or social partners) influence the design of agentic roles and human–agent teams?
- What does “orchestrator” mean in the literature (management, HR, AI) and how is it assessed or developed?
- Are there sector-specific models (financial services, manufacturing, retail) for the Agentic Enterprise that we can compare to our five elements?
