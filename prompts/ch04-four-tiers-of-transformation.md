# Deep Research Prompt: Chapter 4 — The Four Tiers of Transformation

## Purpose of This Prompt

Use this prompt to conduct **extensive external research** on how transformation progresses from Individual → Team → Department → Organisation, and what leadership must do differently at each tier. The goal is to gather evidence, frameworks, and examples for the **research** folder to support writing this chapter.

---

## Chapter Context (What We Already Know)

**Chapter title:** The Four Tiers of Transformation  
**Place in book:** Part II — The Model  
**Target length:** ~11% of book (~3,850 words)  
**Core argument:** Transformation progresses through four tiers (Individual, Team, Department, Organisation); each tier needs different governance, culture, tools, and ROI measurement; the **Individual** tier is where agentic employees are made (AI fluency, skill creation and sharing, compounding network effect).

### Evidence already in our research

- **Tier 1 — Individual:** Personal AI fluency (prompting, validating, ethical use). McKinsey Lilli: 30%+ time saved on information gathering; shift from “AI as search” to “AI as thinking partner.” Governance: personal responsibility (data, hallucinations).
- **Tier 2 — Team:** Superusers, team norms (“Have you asked the AI?”); Google Project Aristotle — psychological safety #1 for team performance; stand-alone agents → coordinating agent groups; “two-in-the-box” (business + tech leads).
- **Tier 3 — Department:** End-to-end workflow reimagination; McKinsey: stand-alone agents → agent groups → agentic swarms; MVOs (Minimum Viable Organisations) — few human overseers. Kore.ai wealth: meeting prep 2–3 hours → 15 minutes; 300% document findability; 22% satisfaction rise.
- **Tier 4 — Organisation:** Enterprise North Star, AI oversight committee, cross-functional task forces; McKinsey example: global retailer, demand/inventory reallocation by agents in seconds; companies with trust-enabling activities 2x more likely to achieve 10%+ revenue growth.
- **Individual as foundation:** Book emphasis: agentic employees are “made” at the individual tier through fluency, skill creation, and sharing — compounding so one person’s innovation becomes organisational capability.
- **Governance/culture/tools/ROI:** Each tier implies different governance, culture, tools, and how ROI is measured (personal productivity → team throughput → department outcomes → enterprise value).

### Book thesis

European CEOs need a clear progression model so they can locate their organisation and invest in the right tier. The chapter must show what “good” looks like at each tier and what leadership must do differently (not one-size-fits-all).

## Main Concepts to Research Deeply (External Focus)

1. **Four-tier (or similar) transformation models**
   - Other frameworks that describe stages from individual to organisation (consultancies, vendors, academia); naming (tiers, levels, stages, waves).
   - How they differ from ours (e.g. three tiers, five stages, maturity curves); what is consistent across sources (e.g. individual first, then team, then broader).
   - Evidence that skipping tiers or focusing only on enterprise-level initiatives without individual/team foundation leads to failure.

2. **Tier 1 — Individual: fluency, skills, network effects**
   - “AI fluency” definitions and assessments; prompt literacy, validation, ethical use; “AI as thinking partner” vs “search tool” — studies or frameworks.
   - Skill creation and sharing: internal “skill” or “capability” marketplaces; reuse of prompts/workflows; how one person’s innovation spreads (network effects, champions, platforms).
   - Governance at individual level: data handling, hallucination awareness, acceptable use; how leading companies train and monitor without stifling experimentation.
   - Data: time saved, tasks augmented, quality of output; ROI at individual tier (productivity, satisfaction, retention).

3. **Tier 2 — Team: superusers, norms, psychological safety**
   - Superuser identification and activation; “two-in-the-box” and business–tech co-leadership; team-level agent coordination (multiple agents, handoffs).
   - Psychological safety and AI adoption: Project Aristotle and follow-ups; team norms (“Have you asked the AI?”); psychological safety and willingness to experiment with AI.
   - Team metrics: collaboration, throughput, error rates, time to decision; pilot-to-production at team level.

4. **Tier 3 — Department: workflows, swarms, MVOs**
   - End-to-end workflow redesign within a function; stand-alone agents → agent groups → swarms; MVOs (minimal human oversight) — definitions, examples, sectors.
   - Department-level ownership: who leads (COO, function head, CDO); budget and prioritisation; how departments avoid becoming silos.
   - Case studies: finance, HR, marketing, operations — full workflow transformation with metrics (cycle time, cost, quality); European examples where possible.

5. **Tier 4 — Organisation: North Star, committees, cross-functional**
   - Enterprise “North Star” for AI; AI oversight or steering committees (composition, mandate, cadence); cross-functional task forces and breaking silos.
   - Enterprise-wide metrics: revenue impact, cost, risk, culture; 2x likelihood of 10%+ revenue growth with trust-enabling activities (McKinsey) — similar studies.
   - Scaling from department to organisation: what breaks, what succeeds; role of CoE, federated models, and executive sponsorship.

6. **Leadership behaviour at each tier**
   - What CEOs and C-suite actually do differently at each tier (time, decisions, messaging, metrics).
   - Typical mistakes: over-investing in Tier 4 before Tier 1/2; treating all tiers with same governance; ignoring individual fluency and skill-sharing as foundation.
   - European context: works councils, collective agreements, or regulatory expectations that affect how tiers are rolled out (e.g. training obligations, information/consultation).

---

## Suggested Research Questions

- Are there validated “tier” or “stage” models for AI/agentic transformation with empirical support (success rates by tier, sequence)?
- How do leading companies measure and reward “skill creation and sharing” at the individual level?
- What is the relationship between psychological safety and AI adoption at team level (surveys, experiments, case studies)?
- What does “Minimum Viable Organisation” (agent swarm with few humans) mean in practice, and in which industries is it emerging?
- How do European firms structure AI oversight at board or ExCo level and link it to tier progression?
- What are the most common reasons transformations stall at Tier 2 or Tier 3, and what do successful companies do differently?
