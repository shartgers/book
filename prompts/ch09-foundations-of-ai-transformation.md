## Main Concepts to Research Deeply (External Focus)

1. **Readiness and maturity frameworks**
   - **Comparison:** Cisco, Microsoft, Gartner, Deloitte, others (2024–2026); pillar/category overlap with our five pillars (Product, People, Process, Technology, Data); strengths and gaps of each.
   - **Scoring and assessment:** How maturity is scored (self-assessment vs audit vs tool); typical dimensions and weightings; benchmarks by industry and geography (European benchmarks if available).
   - **Diagnostic process:** Who runs it (internal vs external); how long; how results are presented to CEO/board; common blind spots (e.g. over-rating tech, under-rating data and people).
   - **European data:** AI Readiness Index 2024 and later editions; country and sector breakdowns; evolution over time.

2. **Five pillars in diagnostic form**
   - **Product & Strategy:** How to assess “product and strategy readiness” (AI in strategy, product roadmap, customer impact); questions or criteria used in practice.
   - **People & Skills:** Talent, roles, upskilling, culture; 6% “meaningful” upskilling vs 89% need; how to measure and score; European talent and training landscape.
   - **Processes & Governance:** Process redesign readiness; governance (policies, risk, compliance); link to EU AI Act and internal controls; how to assess.
   - **Technology & Infrastructure:** Infrastructure, platforms, MCP/skills, security; build vs buy readiness; how to score without over-weighting tech.
   - **Data & Context:** Data quality, availability, governance; “AI-ready data” definitions; 57% not AI-ready (Gartner), 12% truly ready; practical assessment criteria.
   - **Cross-pillar:** Dependencies (e.g. data before models, people before scale); how frameworks handle weighting and sequencing.

3. **Value–Capability–Trust as diagnostic**
   - Using Value/Capability/Trust (Ch 5) to assess readiness: value (where we can create value, pipeline of use cases); capability (people, process, tech to deliver); trust (governance, risk, ethics, compliance).
   - How to present a “three-lens” readiness view to the board or ExCo; linking diagnostic to investment and roadmap priorities.
   - Tensions: high value but low capability or low trust; how to prioritise gap-closing.

4. **From diagnostic to roadmap**
   - Turning readiness scores into a phased roadmap: which gaps to close first; milestones (e.g. “Foundation ready by Q2”); dependencies and critical path.
   - KPIs for “foundations”: not just AI project KPIs but readiness KPIs (e.g. % workforce literate, % data AI-ready, number of governed use cases); link to Ch 10.
   - Roadmap ownership: who owns the roadmap (CEO, CDO, CoE); how it connects to strategic planning and budget cycles.
   - European context: EU AI Act deadlines as fixed milestones; regulatory readiness as a pillar or overlay.

5. **Regulatory and ethical context**
   - EU AI Act as a foundation input: risk classification, compliance timeline, impact on roadmap (e.g. high-risk use cases require specific readiness).
   - Trustworthy AI: how companies integrate ethical principles (fairness, transparency, accountability) into the diagnostic and roadmap; NIST, IBM, Microsoft, EU High-Level Group.
   - Sector-specific: financial services (DORA, etc.), health, critical infrastructure — additional “readiness” dimensions.

6. **Avoiding “deploy in isolation”**
   - What “absorb and act on AI” means: integration with strategy, operating model, and culture vs one-off projects.
   - How readiness diagnostics have been used to stop or redirect projects (e.g. “not ready on data” or “not ready on trust”); examples and lessons.
   - Connection to Ch 8: Foundation phase and readiness; when to run the diagnostic (before Foundation, or as part of Foundation).

## Suggested Research Questions

- What readiness or maturity assessment does your organisation (or a typical European mid-large company) actually run, and what does the output look like?
- How do consultancies recommend going from a readiness score to a roadmap (steps, ownership, timeline)?
- Is there evidence that companies that run a structured diagnostic before scaling AI have better outcomes (success rate, time to value)?
- What are the most common “foundation” gaps in European companies (data, governance, talent, strategy) in recent surveys or reports?
- How do EU AI Act requirements map onto a readiness framework (e.g. as a separate “regulatory readiness” dimension or integrated into Process/Governance)?
- What KPIs do leading companies use to track “readiness” progress (not just project ROI but foundation maturity)?
