# Deep Research Prompt: Chapter 13 — Measuring What Matters

## Purpose of This Prompt

Use this prompt to conduct **extensive external research** on KPIs that reveal real progress, how to avoid pilot purgatory, and the board's evolving governance role. The goal is to gather evidence and frameworks for the **research** folder to support writing this chapter.

---

## Chapter Context (What We Already Know)

**Chapter title:** Measuring What Matters  
**Place in book:** Part IV — The CEO's Playbook  
**Target length:** ~6% of book (~2,700 words)  
**Core argument:** The KPIs that reveal real progress; how to avoid pilot purgatory; the board's evolving governance role.

### Evidence already in our research

- **Pilot purgatory:** McKinsey: nearly 8 in 10 use gen AI but just as many report no significant bottom-line impact; ~90% of transformative use cases stuck in pilot. Bain: micro-productivity trap; grassroots efforts don't self-organise without top direction.
- **KPI categories (research Section 9):** **Financial** (cost savings, revenue impact, ROAI); **Operational** (process efficiency, error reduction, throughput); **Adoption** (tool usage, employee sentiment, workflows redesigned); **Strategic** (CSAT/NPS, decision quality, innovation velocity).
- **Pitfalls:** Pilot tunnel vision; over-indexing on model metrics vs business outcomes; short-term ROI obsession (MIT 95% failure measured ROI at 6 months only); failing to establish baselines. Gartner: 45% of high-maturity orgs keep AI projects operational 3+ years vs 20% low-maturity.
- **Board governance gap:** 66% of boards report limited/minimal/no AI knowledge; only 15% receive AI-related metrics; <25% have board-approved AI policies; 14% discuss AI at every meeting; 88% of organisations use AI in at least one function. McKinsey: boards should set "AI posture," codify governance, demand metrics (ROI by unit, % processes AI-enabled, reskilling progress, regulatory alignment, override rates).
- **ROAI / ROI:** How AI value is measured and attributed; DBS and JPMorgan as rare examples of disclosed total realised AI value; need for business-outcome metrics, not just technical metrics.

### Book thesis

CEOs and boards need to know what to measure and how to govern. This chapter should help them escape pilot purgatory by measuring the right things and give the board a clear role in AI governance.

## Main Concepts to Research Deeply (External Focus)

1. **KPIs that reveal real progress**
   - **Financial:** Cost savings, revenue impact, ROAI; how to attribute value to AI (baselines, control groups, time allocation); pitfalls of short-term ROI (6-month window); when to use NPV or longer horizons.
   - **Operational:** Process efficiency, cycle time, error rate, throughput; function-specific (e.g. marketing conversion, claim handling, code velocity); leading vs lagging indicators.
   - **Adoption:** Usage (daily active, tasks augmented); sentiment and readiness; "workflows redesigned" or "processes AI-enabled" as a metric; superuser and champion metrics.
   - **Strategic:** Customer (NPS, CSAT), decision quality, innovation velocity; linkage to enterprise North Star and strategic goals.
   - **Portfolio view:** Mix of pilots vs scaled; pipeline of use cases; kill criteria for pilots that don't graduate.
   - **European and sector:** Any European benchmarks or sector-specific KPI sets (banking, insurance, manufacturing).

2. **Avoiding pilot purgatory**
   - **Root causes:** Why pilots stay pilots (governance, criteria, ownership, incentives, measurement); Bain "micro-productivity trap"; McKinsey "90% stuck."
   - **Gating and criteria:** Decision process for pilot → production; success criteria defined upfront; who decides and on what data.
   - **Scaling discipline:** "First win" replication; templates and playbooks; resource reallocation from pilot to scale.
   - **Anti-patterns:** Too many pilots; no clear owner; no business outcome metrics; IT-led without business commitment.
   - **Case evidence:** Companies that escaped pilot purgatory (e.g. ING 90% to production); what they did differently; time from pilot to scale.

3. **Board governance: role and metrics**
   - **Current state:** 66% limited/no AI knowledge; 15% receive AI metrics; <25% board-approved AI policies; 14% discuss AI every meeting — sources and more recent data (2025–2026).
   - **Recommended role:** "AI posture"; governance framework; metrics the board should see (McKinsey list: ROI by unit, % processes AI-enabled, reskilling, regulatory alignment, override rates). What else do leading boards do?
   - **Committee structures:** Audit, risk, technology, or dedicated AI committee; composition (independent, C-suite, external); cadence and agenda.
   - **Board literacy:** Training and briefings; how boards that "get it" differ; CEO and C-suite role in board education.
   - **Regulatory and liability:** EU AI Act and board accountability; NFRD/CSRD if relevant; directors' duties and AI risk.
   - **European context:** Corporate governance codes; two-tier boards (e.g. Germany); works council or stakeholder reporting that touches AI.

4. **Baselines, attribution, and rigour**
   - **Baselines:** Why many AI projects fail to prove impact (no baseline, wrong counterfactual); how to establish baselines before pilot.
   - **Attribution:** Incremental impact of AI vs other factors; A/B or control groups; when to use proxies (e.g. time saved, throughput) vs revenue/cost.
   - **Rigour:** MIT 95% failure and 6-month measurement; Gartner 45% vs 20% project longevity by maturity; recommendation for measurement horizon and review cadence.

5. **Connections to other chapters**
   - **Ch 8 (Value/Capability/Trust):** How Value/Capability/Trust translates into metrics (value = financial/strategic; capability = adoption, skills; trust = compliance, override, incidents).
   - **Ch 11 (Playbook):** KPIs by phase (Foundation: literacy, governance; Activation: strategy setting with AI Transformation Framework, pilot success, first wins; Scale: enterprise value).
   - **Ch 12 (Readiness):** Readiness KPIs vs transformation KPIs; roadmap milestones as measurable outcomes.

## Suggested Research Questions

- What exact KPIs do companies that have scaled AI (e.g. DBS, ING, BBVA, Allianz) report internally or disclose externally?
- What does the board actually receive at AI-leading companies (dashboard, narrative, frequency), and who presents it?
- Is there research on the relationship between "clear success criteria and gating" and pilot-to-production rates?
- How do you attribute financial impact to AI when multiple initiatives and external factors are at play?
- What do corporate governance codes or regulators (EU, UK, national) say or imply about board duties regarding AI?
- What are the most common mistakes in measuring AI success (and how to avoid them) according to consultancies or practitioners?
