# Deep Research Prompt: Chapter 12 — Readiness

## Purpose of This Prompt

Use this prompt to conduct **extensive external research** on readiness diagnostics and tools for organisational assessment: where the organisation is, what to fix first, and how to prioritise before scaling. The goal is to gather frameworks, tools, and examples for the **research** folder to support writing this short, practical chapter.

---

## Chapter Context (What We Already Know)

**Chapter title:** Readiness  
**Place in book:** Part IV — The CEO's Playbook  
**Target length:** ~4% of book (~1,800 words)  
**Core argument:** Readiness is not a one-time audit but a disciplined way to prioritise: what to fix first, which gaps block progress, and how to sequence investments so the organisation can absorb and act on AI rather than deploy it in isolation. This chapter lays out readiness diagnostics and assessment tools so leaders can make informed decisions about what to do next.

### Evidence already in our research

- **Ch 7 (Foundations):** Covers the foundational layer (data, technology, integration, enablement) and introduces diagnostic thinking; Ch 12 focuses specifically on *readiness as a decision tool* — diagnostics, tools, and prioritisation.
- **Ch 8 (Value, Capabilities, Trust):** The three lenses can be used as readiness dimensions (are we ready on value pipeline? capability? trust?).
- **Readiness frameworks:** Cisco AI Readiness Index, Microsoft maturity, Gartner, Deloitte; AI Readiness Index 2024 (Western Europe 69.56); >60% European firms at earliest stages.
- **Research:** `research/ai-readiness-framework.md`, `research/book-research-claude.md` — prerequisites, five pillars, Value/Capability/Trust, diagnostic, roadmap.
- **Link to playbook:** Ch 11 (Building) references Foundation → Activation → Scale; readiness diagnostics help leaders know *when* they are ready to move from Foundation to Activation and what to fix first.

### Book thesis

CEOs need a clear, actionable view of readiness: not a long report but a way to answer "where are we, what do we fix first, and how do we prioritise before scaling?" This chapter provides the diagnostics and tools.

## Main Concepts to Research Deeply (External Focus)

1. **Readiness diagnostics and tools**
   - Types of assessments: self-assessment, audit, tool-based; typical dimensions (strategy, data, technology, talent, governance, culture); how long they take and who runs them.
   - Outputs: scorecards, heat maps, gap lists; how to present to CEO/board without overwhelming; "what to fix first" prioritisation methods.
   - Benchmarks: industry, geography (European), company size; how to interpret scores (e.g. "below 60% on data = fix before scaling").

2. **Prioritisation before scaling**
   - How to go from "we have gaps" to "we fix X first, then Y"; dependency ordering (e.g. data before models, governance before scale); critical path.
   - Link to Value/Capability/Trust (Ch 8): using the three lenses to prioritise readiness investments.
   - Avoiding "readiness as delay": when to run lightweight vs full diagnostic; how to avoid using readiness as an excuse not to start.

3. **Tools and templates**
   - Checklists, scorecards, or lightweight tools that CEOs or ExCo can use (or delegate); examples from consultancies or practitioners.
   - Connection to Ch 11 playbook: readiness checkpoints at Foundation → Activation and Activation → Scale.

4. **European context**
   - EU AI Act and regulatory readiness as a dimension; how readiness assessments incorporate compliance and trust.
   - Short chapter: keep focused on diagnostics and prioritisation; avoid duplicating Ch 7 (Foundations) depth.

## Suggested Research Questions

- What readiness or diagnostic tools do consultancies and companies actually use (name, format, duration), and what does the output look like?
- How do leading companies prioritise "what to fix first" after a readiness assessment (framework, criteria, sequencing)?
- Is there evidence that companies that run a structured readiness diagnostic before scaling have better outcomes?
- What are the most common readiness gaps in European companies (data, governance, talent, strategy) in recent surveys?
