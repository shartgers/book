# Deep Research Prompt: Chapter 8 — Value, Capabilities, Trust

## Purpose of This Prompt

Use this prompt to conduct **extensive external research** on the three-angle decision framework (Value, Capabilities, Trust) for evaluating AI investments and setting priorities. The goal is to gather evidence, frameworks, and examples for the **research** folder to support writing this chapter.

---

## Chapter Context (What We Already Know)

**Chapter title:** Value, Capabilities, Trust  
**Place in book:** Part II — The Model  
**Target length:** ~8% of book (~3,600 words)  
**Core argument:** A three-angle decision framework for every AI investment and for setting the right priorities: **Value** (where we create value), **Capabilities** (what we need to deliver), **Trust** (governance, risk, ethics).

### Evidence already in our research

- **Value:** BCG Deploy–Reshape–Invent (DRI): **Deploy** = off-the-shelf, 10–15% productivity; **Reshape** = end-to-end AI workflows in functions; **Invent** = new business models and revenue. BCG: AI-mature companies get 72% of AI value in core functions (operations, marketing, sales). Roland Berger PE: procurement AI 7% savings on indirect spend in <10 weeks; dynamic pricing $5M margin in one quarter; AI forecasting 25% error reduction.
- **Capabilities:** Cross-functional teams, agile operating model, experimentation culture; new roles (Agent Orchestrator, AI Product Manager, MLOps); McKinsey: "domain leader" (N-2/N-3) as "probably the single most critical role"; only 6% upskilling "meaningfully" vs 89% acknowledging need.
- **Trust:** IBM five Pillars, Microsoft six Principles, Deloitte Trustworthy AI, NIST AI RMF; **EU AI Act** as defining for Europe (risk-based: unacceptable, high-risk, limited, minimal); penalties €35M or 7% global turnover. Allianz: EU AI Pact Nov 2024 — proactive compliance as trust moat. Gartner: 57% high-maturity orgs have business units that trust and use AI vs 14% low-maturity.
- **Book role:** This framework is a differentiator; we use it for prioritisation and for diagnosing readiness (see Ch 12). European angle: Trust is not optional compliance but competitive advantage.

### Book thesis

European CEOs need a simple, memorable lens to evaluate every AI initiative and to align the organisation. Value/Capabilities/Trust should become a recurring filter in the book (strategy, readiness, board, playbook).

## Main Concepts to Research Deeply (External Focus)

1. **Value: creation, measurement, and prioritisation**
   - Beyond DRI: other value taxonomies (e.g. cost vs revenue vs risk; short-term vs long-term; core vs support).
   - ROAI / ROI on AI: how it is defined and measured; pitfalls (short-term only, pilot ROI vs scaled ROI); MIT "95% failure" and 6-month measurement window.
   - Where value actually appears: core vs support functions; industry breakdowns (financial services, manufacturing, retail, public sector); European data if available.
   - Prioritisation: how leading companies rank use cases (value vs effort vs risk); portfolio view (Deploy vs Reshape vs Invent mix).
   - Quantified value cases: more examples like Roland Berger (procurement, pricing, forecasting) with attribution and timeframes.

2. **Capabilities: people, process, technology**
   - "Capabilities" as used by strategy/consulting (e.g. capability-based strategy); mapping to AI: data, talent, process, tech, governance.
   - Critical roles: Agent Orchestrator, AI Product Manager, MLOps, domain leader (N-2/N-3); job market data, skills frameworks, training curricula.
   - 10/20/70 (BCG): 70% people and process — breakdown of what "70%" means in practice (change management, workflow redesign, incentives, culture).
   - Capability gaps: 6% "meaningful" upskilling vs 89% need; how to assess capability readiness and close gaps; European talent and training landscape.
   - Cross-functional and agile: how AI initiatives are resourced (dedicated teams, embedded vs central); failure modes when capabilities are missing.

3. **Trust: governance, risk, ethics, regulation**
   - Trust frameworks: IBM, Microsoft, Deloitte, NIST; comparison and overlap; "trustworthy AI" vs "responsible AI" vs "ethical AI" — definitions and adoption.
   - EU AI Act in practice: risk classification, high-risk obligations, transparency, fundamental rights impact assessments; how companies are implementing (surveys, case studies, legal/consulting analyses); first enforcement or guidance (2025–2026).
   - Trust as advantage: Allianz EU AI Pact; "trust moat"; customer and employee trust in AI; data linking trust investments to adoption or commercial outcomes.
   - Board and audit: how trust is overseen (board committee, risk committee, dedicated AI governance); reporting and metrics for trust.
   - European specifics: GDPR overlap, sector rules (finance, health, critical infrastructure), national implementations of the AI Act.

4. **Using the three angles together**
   - Decision frameworks that combine value, capability, and risk/trust (e.g. 2×2 matrices, scorecards, gating criteria).
   - Tensions: high-value but low capability or high risk; how organisations trade off and sequence (e.g. quick wins vs capability-building vs trust-building).
   - Link to readiness (Ch 12): Value/Capability/Trust as diagnostic dimensions; how they feed into roadmap and milestones.
   - Quotes or frameworks from CEOs/consultants on "we evaluate every AI initiative on value, capability, and trust."

5. **European angle**
   - European-specific value data (productivity, revenue, cost) and capability data (talent, training, roles).
   - Trust as differentiator: regulation, consumer and employee expectations, "trust premium" in B2B or B2C in Europe.
   - PE and mid-market: how Value/Capability/Trust is applied in due diligence or value-creation plans (we have EQT, FTI; more examples welcome).

## Suggested Research Questions

- What alternative "triple" or "multi-criteria" frameworks exist for AI investment (beyond Value/Capability/Trust)?
- How do leading companies formally score or gate AI initiatives (e.g. value score, capability readiness, risk level)?
- What is the state of EU AI Act implementation in 2025–2026: deadlines, guidance, early enforcement, and practical challenges?
- Is there evidence that "trust" (governance, transparency, ethics) correlates with higher adoption, revenue, or retention in Europe?
- How do PE or mid-market players use value/capability/trust in their AI playbooks and portfolio company support?
- What does "domain leader" (N-2/N-3) do in practice in successful transformations, and how is this role defined and rewarded?
