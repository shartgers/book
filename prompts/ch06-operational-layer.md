The goal is to gather evidence and frameworks for the **research** folder to support writing this chapter on how to structure teams and roles for agentic work—from task-doer to orchestrator at scale.

## Main Concepts to Research Deeply (External Focus)

1. **Roles and skills for agentic work**
   - New roles: Agent Orchestrator, AI Product Manager, domain leader (N-2/N-3); job definitions, skills frameworks, and how leading companies structure these roles.
   - Shift from task-doer to orchestrator: what it means in practice; training and literacy; "Have you asked the AI?" norms; superusers and champions.
   - Skills: reusable workflows (skills), sharing, and compounding; how to assess and close capability gaps (link to Ch 8 Capabilities, Ch 12 Readiness).

2. **Process and org design**
   - Process redesign for agentic workflows: end-to-end vs bolt-on; zero-based process design; stand-alone agents → agent groups → agentic swarms (McKinsey).
   - Org design: centralised vs hub-and-spoke vs embedded; two-in-the-box (business + tech); span of control when managers orchestrate many agents; MVOs (Minimum Viable Organisations).
   - Failure modes: over-centralisation; under-investment in people/process (70% in BCG 10/20/70); pilot success that doesn't scale because the operating model wasn't changed.

3. **European and sector context**
   - Works council, labour, and social dialogue implications for role and process change in Europe.
   - Sector examples: banking (ING, BBVA), insurance (Allianz), industrial (Siemens, Schneider) — how they describe their operational layer (roles, process, org design).

4. **Connection to other chapters**
   - Ch 3 (agentic employee, three profiles); Ch 4 (tiers); Ch 5 (framework image); Ch 11 (Foundation/Activation/Scale and when to invest in the Operational layer).

## Suggested Research Questions

- What roles do consultancies and leading companies define for "agentic" or "AI-augmented" organisations (Agent Orchestrator, AI Product Manager, etc.), and what do they do in practice?
- How do organisations redesign process (not just automate) for agentic workflows? What frameworks or steps are recommended?
- What org design patterns (centralised, hub-and-spoke, embedded) work best for AI transformation, and when do companies transition between them?
- How do European companies handle works council and labour implications when changing roles and process for AI?
