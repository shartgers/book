# The 2028 Global Intelligence Crisis — Key Insights

**Source:** [Citrini Research](https://www.citriniresearch.com/p/2028gic)
**Authors:** James Van Geelen (CEO, Citrini Research) & Alap Shah (AI entrepreneur/investor, ex-Citadel)
**Published:** February 2026
**Format:** Speculative macro scenario — framed as a "Memo from June 2028"
**Note:** This is a stress-test/thought experiment, not a prediction. It went viral on Wall Street and in tech circles and prompted a formal rebuttal from Citadel Securities.

---

## What Is It?

A viral financial essay that asks: *what happens if AI advances faster than the economy can absorb?* Written as a fictional memo from June 2028, it traces a plausible path from AI-driven productivity euphoria in 2026 to a full systemic financial crisis by mid-2028. It generated significant coverage in Bloomberg, Fortune, Yahoo Finance, and Seeking Alpha — and provoked a White House economist to call it "science fiction."

## Phase 1: The AI Euphoria (2025–2026)

- Agentic coding tools took a **step-function jump** in capability in late 2025
- Initial white-collar layoffs began in early 2026 — margins expanded, earnings beat, stocks rallied
- S&P 500 flirted with 8,000 by October 2026; Nasdaq broke 30,000
- Nominal GDP printed mid-to-high single-digit annualized growth
- Real output per hour rose at rates not seen since the 1950s
- Corporate profits hit records and were reinvested entirely into more AI compute

---

## Phase 2: The Intelligence Displacement Spiral

The central analytical framework of the report. A self-reinforcing feedback loop with no natural brake:

> AI capability improves → companies need fewer workers → white-collar layoffs increase → displaced workers spend less → margin pressure pushes firms to invest more in AI → AI capability improves further

Key characteristics:
- **"Phantom GDP":** Corporate book profits grow while aggregate consumer demand contracts — GDP looks healthy while the middle class hollows out
- Owners of compute see wealth explode as labor costs vanish
- Real wage growth collapses despite record productivity headlines
- White-collar workers — the highest earners and biggest spenders — are the primary displacement target

> *"You can cut rates to zero and buy every MBS and all the defaulted software LBO debt in the market… it won't change the fact that a Claude agent can do the work of a $180,000 product manager for $200/month."*

---

## Phase 3: The Mortgage Market ($13 Trillion at Risk)

- White-collar incomes are the foundation of the **$13 trillion US mortgage market**
- As structural unemployment hits knowledge workers, underwriters are forced to reassess prime mortgage creditworthiness
- Home prices in tech hubs (San Francisco, Austin) begin to collapse as high-earning professionals face permanent displacement
- Delinquencies rise — not yet a 2008-level crisis, but the trajectory is what matters

---

## Phase 4: Private Credit & SaaS Collapse ($2.5 Trillion Market)

- During the AI boom of 2025, vast capital was deployed into software and technology LBOs at valuations assuming perpetual ARR growth
- A competent developer + Claude Code/Codex can **replicate core SaaS functionality in weeks** — destroying the subscription model
- The "long tail of SaaS" (Monday.com, Zapier, Asana) gets hit first — expected, even priced in
- Then the **systems of record** fall — Salesforce, Workday, SAP — which were supposed to be safe
- First wave of PE-backed software defaults hits in mid-2027
- Marks in private credit become wildly disconnected from public market reality
- The $2.5 trillion private credit market faces a systemic reckoning

---

## Phase 5: Financial Intermediation Collapse

- AI agents bypass the "friction layer" that most financial businesses are built on
- A hypothetical Mastercard report in April 2027 reports "agent-led price optimisation" and "pressure in discretionary categories" — purchase volume growth slows
- Card-focused businesses (American Express, Capital One) see their model eroded as AI eliminates the human inefficiencies they monetise
- The broader thesis: the US economy has spent **50 years building a massive rent-extraction layer on top of human limitations** — that layer is now being disintermediated

---

## Phase 6: Global Spillover — India

- India's IT services sector (TCS, Infosys, Wipro) faces structural headwinds as clients internalize development work
- Contract cancellations accelerate
- The rupee depreciates **18% against the dollar** in months
- Preliminary IMF discussions begin in early 2028

---

## Policy Impotence

Traditional tools (rate cuts, QE, MBS purchases) can address the **financial** engine of the crisis — but cannot address the **real economy** engine, because the root cause is not tight financial conditions. It is AI making human intelligence less scarce and less valuable.

The lag between economic reality and policy response — always a problem — becomes dangerously wide when the underlying driver is technological, not cyclical.

---

## The Citadel Securities Rebuttal

Ken Griffin's market-making arm published a formal rebuttal, arguing the scenario reflects a misunderstanding of macroeconomic fundamentals and technology adoption curves:

- **Labor demand rising:** Indeed data shows software engineer postings up **11% YoY** in early 2026
- **AI adoption plateau:** Real Time Population Survey (St. Louis Fed) shows daily generative AI use for work is "unexpectedly stable" with "little evidence of imminent displacement risk"
- **New business formation expanding** alongside data center construction boom driving hiring
- **S-curve adoption:** Technology diffusion historically follows a slow-accelerate-plateau pattern, not a vertical ramp
- **Compute constraints:** Displacing white-collar work at scale would require orders of magnitude more compute — which would drive up compute costs, creating a natural brake

---

## Citrini's Conclusion

The "repricing" of human intelligence is painful, disorderly, and far from complete — but not necessarily a signal of total collapse. A new equilibrium is possible, but only if institutions and investors adapt to a world where the most productive asset (AI) produces fewer jobs, not more.

> *"The window to assess which assumptions will survive the decade is narrowing."*

---

## Relevance to the Book

This article is directly relevant to several themes:

- **IT spend migration:** The scenario quantifies how SaaS subscription spend migrates to token-based AI usage — and at what speed
- **White-collar displacement:** The knowledge workers most at risk are the same people who need to become "agentic employees"
- **Skills as competitive moat:** The workers and organisations that encode expertise into reusable skills (not just use AI as a tool) are better positioned to survive the repricing
- **Lead from the top:** The policy/leadership vacuum in the scenario is a warning — organisations that wait for clarity before acting will be caught in the spiral
- **Unlimited tokens as retention:** In a world where a Claude agent does a $180k PM job for $200/month, the humans who remain employed will be those who can direct, build, and manage agents — access to tokens is how you develop that capability
