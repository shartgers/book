**This document summarises a speculative thought experiment, not established facts.** The Citrini report is explicitly framed as a stress-test and hypothetical scenario — a "Memo from June 2028" — designed to explore *what could happen if* AI advances faster than the economy can absorb. All numbers, projections, and causal claims below refer to this hypothetical scenario only. When quoting or citing this material, it must be explicitly attributed as a hypothetical prediction, not as factual data.

## What the Hypothetical Scenario Explores

The essay asks: *what happens if AI advances faster than the economy can absorb?* It traces a plausible (but speculative) path from AI-driven productivity euphoria in 2026 to a full systemic financial crisis by mid-2028. The scenario is intended as a provocation for stress-testing assumptions, not as a factual forecast.

## Phase 1: The AI Euphoria (2025–2026) — Hypothetical

In this scenario:

- Agentic coding tools take a step-function jump in capability in late 2025
- Initial white-collar layoffs begin in early 2026; margins expand, earnings beat, stocks rally
- S&P 500 flirts with 8,000 by October 2026; Nasdaq breaks 30,000
- Nominal GDP prints mid-to-high single-digit annualized growth
- Real output per hour rises at rates not seen since the 1950s
- Corporate profits hit records and are reinvested entirely into more AI compute

---

## Phase 2: The Intelligence Displacement Spiral — Hypothetical Framework

The scenario's central analytical framework: a self-reinforcing feedback loop with no natural brake:

> *In this hypothetical:* AI capability improves → companies need fewer workers → white-collar layoffs increase → displaced workers spend less → margin pressure pushes firms to invest more in AI → AI capability improves further

Key concepts in the scenario:

- **"Phantom GDP":** Corporate book profits grow while aggregate consumer demand contracts — GDP looks healthy while the middle class hollows out
- Owners of compute see wealth explode as labor costs vanish
- Real wage growth collapses despite record productivity headlines
- White-collar workers — the highest earners and biggest spenders — are the primary displacement target

> *Hypothetical quote from the scenario:* "You can cut rates to zero and buy every MBS and all the defaulted software LBO debt in the market… it won't change the fact that a Claude agent can do the work of a $180,000 product manager for $200/month."

## Phase 3: The Mortgage Market — Hypothetical ($13 Trillion at Risk)

In the scenario:

- White-collar incomes are the foundation of the $13 trillion US mortgage market
- As structural unemployment hits knowledge workers, underwriters reassess prime mortgage creditworthiness
- Home prices in tech hubs (San Francisco, Austin) begin to collapse as high-earning professionals face permanent displacement
- Delinquencies rise — not yet a 2008-level crisis in the scenario, but the trajectory is what matters

---

## Phase 4: Private Credit & SaaS Collapse — Hypothetical ($2.5 Trillion Market)

In this hypothetical:

- During the AI boom of 2025, vast capital is deployed into software and technology LBOs at valuations assuming perpetual ARR growth
- A competent developer + Claude Code/Codex can replicate core SaaS functionality in weeks — destroying the subscription model
- The "long tail of SaaS" (Monday.com, Zapier, Asana) gets hit first
- Then the systems of record fall — Salesforce, Workday, SAP — which were supposed to be safe
- First wave of PE-backed software defaults hits in mid-2027
- Marks in private credit become wildly disconnected from public market reality
- The $2.5 trillion private credit market faces a systemic reckoning

---

## Phase 5: Financial Intermediation Collapse — Hypothetical

In the scenario:

- AI agents bypass the "friction layer" that most financial businesses are built on
- A hypothetical Mastercard report in April 2027 reports "agent-led price optimisation" and "pressure in discretionary categories"
- Card-focused businesses (American Express, Capital One) see their model eroded as AI eliminates the human inefficiencies they monetise
- The broader thesis: the US economy has spent 50 years building a massive rent-extraction layer on top of human limitations — that layer is now being disintermediated

## Phase 6: Global Spillover — India (Hypothetical)

In this hypothetical scenario:

- India's IT services sector (TCS, Infosys, Wipro) faces structural headwinds as clients internalise development work
- Contract cancellations accelerate
- The rupee depreciates 18% against the dollar in months
- Preliminary IMF discussions begin in early 2028

## Policy Impotence — Hypothetical Argument

The scenario argues that traditional tools (rate cuts, QE, MBS purchases) can address the *financial* engine of the crisis — but cannot address the *real economy* engine, because the root cause is not tight financial conditions. It is AI making human intelligence less scarce and less valuable.

The lag between economic reality and policy response becomes dangerously wide when the underlying driver is technological, not cyclical.

## The Citadel Securities Rebuttal (Factual)

Ken Griffin's market-making arm published a formal rebuttal, arguing the scenario reflects a misunderstanding of macroeconomic fundamentals and technology adoption curves:

- **Labor demand rising:** Indeed data shows software engineer postings up 11% YoY in early 2026
- **AI adoption plateau:** Real Time Population Survey (St. Louis Fed) shows daily generative AI use for work is "unexpectedly stable" with "little evidence of imminent displacement risk"
- **New business formation expanding** alongside data center construction boom driving hiring
- **S-curve adoption:** Technology diffusion historically follows a slow-accelerate-plateau pattern, not a vertical ramp
- **Compute constraints:** Displacing white-collar work at scale would require orders of magnitude more compute — which would drive up compute costs, creating a natural brake

---

## Citrini's Conclusion (Hypothetical Framing)

The scenario concludes that the "repricing" of human intelligence could be painful, disorderly, and far from complete — but not necessarily a signal of total collapse. A new equilibrium is possible, but only if institutions and investors adapt to a world where the most productive asset (AI) produces fewer jobs, not more.

> *Hypothetical quote:* "The window to assess which assumptions will survive the decade is narrowing."

---

## Relevance to the Book (Conceptual, Not Factual)

The hypothetical scenario is relevant to several themes as a *conceptual* lens:

- **IT spend migration:** The scenario illustrates how SaaS subscription spend could migrate to token-based AI usage — and at what speed, *if* the scenario were to unfold
- **White-collar displacement:** The knowledge workers most at risk in the scenario are the same people who may need to become "agentic employees"
- **Skills as competitive moat:** The workers and organisations that encode expertise into reusable skills (not just use AI as a tool) may be better positioned to survive a repricing
- **Lead from the top:** The policy/leadership vacuum in the scenario is a warning — organisations that wait for clarity before acting could be caught in such a spiral
- **Unlimited tokens as retention:** In a world where a Claude agent hypothetically does a $180k PM job for $200/month, the humans who remain employed would be those who can direct, build, and manage agents — access to tokens is how you develop that capability
