## Company Case Material

### 1. BBVA (Spain) — Enterprise-Wide AI Banking Transformation

**Chronology and rollout:**
BBVA became the first European bank to sign a strategic agreement with OpenAI. The ChatGPT Enterprise rollout was phased: 3,000 employees in the initial pilot, expanded to 11,000, and then deployed to all 120,000 employees across 25 countries. The decision was CEO- and board-led: 250 senior leaders were trained, including CEO Onur Genç and Chairman Carlos Torres Vila personally.
*(Sources: OpenAI case study "From pilot to practice: How BBVA is scaling AI across the organization," 2025; BBVA press releases 2024–2025)*

**Dilemma — Scale vs. control:**
BBVA's central dilemma was how to put a powerful generative AI tool in the hands of 120,000 employees across 25 countries without losing control. Their answer: align security, legal, and compliance teams from day one and create a "safe place" for experimentation — reducing shadow AI risk by making the official tool better and more accessible than any workaround.

**Metrics (with sources and dates):**
- ~3 hours saved per employee per week *(OpenAI case study, 2025)*
- 83% weekly active usage rate *(OpenAI case study, 2025)*
- 20,000+ custom GPTs created, ~4,000 in frequent use *(OpenAI, 2025)*
- Peru-based assistant used by 3,000+ employees: query handling time reduced from 7.5 min to 1 min (~87% reduction) *(BBVA press, 2025)*
- Legal Assistant GPT handles 40,000 annual client questions, supporting a team of 9 lawyers *(OpenAI, 2025)*
- Credit Analysis Pro GPT automates data analysis for risk assessments *(OpenAI, 2025)*

**AI Factory and embedded talent:**
BBVA's AI Factory employs ~400 professionals operating through local hubs in Spain, Mexico, and Turkey. The factory is a multidisciplinary unit combining data science, ML engineering, solution architecture, data analytics, and product. In addition, ~300 "AI wizards" are embedded within business units — bridging central capability with domain expertise. BBVA has surpassed 1,000 data scientists across the organisation.
*(Sources: BBVA AI Factory website; BBVA press "BBVA surpasses 1,000 data scientists," 2025)*

**Leadership quotes:**
- Carlos Torres Vila (Chairman): *"We were pioneers in the digital and mobile transformation, and we are now entering the AI era with even greater ambition."*
- BBVA also co-developed with IBM a stress test for discriminatory bias in generative AI models for non-English languages, presented at NeurIPS — compliance-driven research producing genuine innovation.

**European angle:**
Spanish banking market; EU regulatory context; BBVA's bias-testing research addresses EU AI Act fairness requirements proactively.

### 2. ING (Netherlands) — Pragmatic AI at Scale

**Chronology and approach:**
ING built a 500-strong analytics team and achieved a 90% pilot-to-production rate (versus the industry average of ~30%). COO Marnix van Stiphout describes the approach as "conservatively aggressive" — focused exploration within strict governance boundaries. All AI development is concentrated in five strategic domains under COO oversight, with an AI Ethics Committee including the CEO reviewing each use case.
*(Sources: McKinsey interview with van Stiphout, 2024; Computer Weekly interviews, 2024–2025; Ziptone, 2024)*

**Five strategic AI domains:**
1. Know Your Customer (KYC) and compliance
2. Customer service chatbots and straight-through processing
3. Wholesale banking lending and sustainability support
4. Hyper-personalised retail marketing
5. Software engineering and tech productivity
*(Source: McKinsey interview with van Stiphout)*

**Dilemma — Central vs. decentralised AI:**
ING's dilemma was whether to let AI bloom organically across business units or centralise it. Van Stiphout chose centralisation explicitly: *"Without this governance... we might have seen bits of AI all over the place."* The centralised platform approach — standardised cloud-hosted models, centralised risk controls, guardrails, and real-time monitoring — enabled deployment across multiple countries while maintaining strong business connections.

**Metrics (with sources and dates):**
- 90% pilot-to-production rate vs. ~30% industry average *(Ziptone, 2024; Computer Weekly, 2025)*
- 75% of customer queries automated via AI chatbots *(ING Newsroom, 2024)*
- 25% productivity gains when AI introduced to operations *(McKinsey/van Stiphout interview, 2024)*
- Google partnership detecting 2–4x more financial crime while reducing false positives by 60% *(Ch9 research)*
- 5,000 employees trained on data fluency and GenAI *(Computer Weekly, 2025)*
- Employees interact with AI tools 30–50 times per week in some markets *(Computer Weekly, 2025)*
- Customer due diligence reduced from weeks to seconds *(Ch9 research)*

**Leadership quotes:**
- Bahadir Yilmaz (Chief Analytics Officer): *"The conversation is no longer about if AI works. It's about understanding where it works and, more importantly, where it matters to the client."*
- Marnix van Stiphout (COO): *"Without this governance... we might have seen bits of AI all over the place."*
- Yilmaz: *"We are the largest mortgage provider in Europe. We have domain knowledge that can be transferred into digital workflows, supported by AI."*

**European angle:**
Netherlands/EU context; ING's governance-first model aligns with EU AI Act expectations; strong ecosystem around Eindhoven (ASML, NXP, Philips); high digital infrastructure maturity.

### 3. Siemens (Germany) — Industrial AI Leader

**Chronology and strategy:**
Siemens won the Hermes Award 2025 for its Industrial Copilot at Hannover Messe — the most prestigious industrial innovation award in Europe. CEO Roland Busch has made industrial AI a central pillar of Siemens' strategy. Cedrik Neike, CEO of Digital Industries, drives execution. Siemens distinguishes sharply between consumer AI and industrial AI: consumer AI cannot simply be deployed on factory floors.
*(Sources: Siemens press releases, 2025; Top Company Guide interview with Neike)*

**Nanjing Digital Native Factory (key showcase):**
Siemens' first "Digital Native Factory" in Nanjing, China — named a World Economic Forum Global Lighthouse Factory. Built entirely digital-first: every component designed, tested, and optimised virtually before physical construction.

**Metrics (with sources and dates):**
- Production capacity increased by nearly 200% *(Siemens press, 2024)*
- Production efficiency improved by 20% *(Siemens press, 2024)*
- Lead times cut by 78% *(WEF Lighthouse recognition, 2024)*
- Time-to-market reduced by 33% (45-day delivery windows to 10 days) *(WEF/Siemens, 2024)*
- Field failures fell 46% *(WEF/Siemens, 2024)*
- Carbon emissions decreased by 28% *(WEF/Siemens, 2024)*
- Amberg plant: 20% reduction in downtime through predictive maintenance *(existing research)*
- 50+ AI applications deployed in Nanjing factory *(Siemens press)*
- Nanjing is the fifth Siemens site to achieve WEF Lighthouse status

**Dilemma — Industrial safety vs. speed; build vs. partner:**
Siemens' dilemma was whether to build its own industrial AI models or partner. The answer: both. Siemens partnered with NVIDIA to build the "Industrial AI Operating System," with Microsoft for the Industrial Copilot, and with Accenture (7,000-expert joint business group). But the core argument is that industrial AI requires domain-specific, multimodal models — not repurposed consumer AI.

**AI agents expansion (May 2025):**
Siemens announced advanced AI agents for industrial automation — shifting from question-answer copilots to autonomous agents that proactively execute entire industrial workflows. Target: up to 50% productivity gains for industrial companies. A future marketplace of industrial AI agents on the Siemens Xcelerator platform.
*(Source: BusinessWire / Siemens press, May 2025)*

**Leadership quotes:**
- Cedrik Neike: *"To unlock the full potential of AI in industry, we need multimodal, industrial-grade foundation models — built to understand machines, workflows, and real-world constraints."*
- Neike: AI is a *"supercharger for the digital transformation"* needed to address aging populations, skilled worker shortages, production localisation, and sustainability.
- Roland Busch called the EU AI Act *"a key reason Europe is lagging"* but signed the EU AI Pact regardless — wanting smarter regulation, not no regulation.

**European angle:**
Germany/DACH; Industry 4.0 originated in Germany (2011), now evolving into AI-native manufacturing; Mittelstand represents both opportunity and challenge; NVIDIA building world's first industrial AI cloud for European manufacturers in Germany (10,000 Blackwell GPUs).

### 4. Allianz (Germany) — Insurance AI at Scale

**Chronology and strategy:**
Allianz ranks #2 globally in the Evident AI Insurance Index (June 2025), behind AXA. Allianz leads the index in the Talent pillar — the largest AI headcount among the 30 indexed insurers. The company employs ~10% of the entire AI workforce across the 30 major insurers assessed. COO Barbara Karuth-Zelle drives the transformation with a clear philosophy: *"Digital and AI transformations are not mainly about the technology but rather the mindset, the people, and the organization."*
*(Sources: Evident AI Insurance Index, June 2025; McKinsey interview with Karuth-Zelle; Allianz media centre)*

**Project Nemo (key showcase):**
Launched in Australia in July 2025 for food spoilage claims processing. Seven specialised AI agents handle planning, coverage checks, weather validation, fraud screening, payout calculation, and audit — while keeping humans in the final decision-making loop. Simple claims under AUD$500 processed within hours rather than days. Built and deployed in under 100 days. Allianz is now exploring expansion to travel delays, simple auto claims, and property damage assessments.
*(Source: CompleteAITraining.com; Insurance News Australia, 2025)*

**Metrics (with sources and dates):**
- #2 Evident AI Insurance Index globally *(Evident, June 2025)*
- ~10% of AI workforce across 30 major insurers *(Evident, June 2025)*
- Close to 400 GenAI use cases *(Allianz media centre, 2025)*
- Project Nemo: 80% reduction in claim processing and settlement time *(Allianz, 2025)*
- Project Nemo: 7-agent workflow executing in under 5 minutes *(Allianz, 2025)*
- 12,000+ employees completed AI upskilling program in 2024 *(Allianz, 2024)*
- Signed EU AI Pact, November 2024 *(public record)*
- Partnership with Anthropic (January 2026) co-developing systems that log every decision, rationale, and data source *(Ch9 research)*

**Dilemma — Mindset over technology:**
Allianz's dilemma was not which technology to choose but how to shift the organisational mindset. Karuth-Zelle's three pillars: (1) human-centric approach aligned with EU AI Act; (2) employee engagement through reskilling — including an internal ChatGPT instance with data confidentiality protections; (3) scaling key use cases across claims, underwriting, marketing, and software development.

**Leadership quotes:**
- Barbara Karuth-Zelle (COO): *"Digital and AI transformations are not mainly about the technology but rather the mindset, the people, and the organization."*
- Karuth-Zelle: Success requires *"sharing mindset, curiosity, creativity, and can-do attitude"* combined with agile working and diverse teams.
- Manuela Diviach (Head of Group Operations): The company maintains *"a disciplined approach to prioritising innovative AI projects, guided by the core principle of value creation."*

**European angle:**
German insurance sector; EU AI Pact early signatory; Anthropic partnership for auditable AI; European insurers hold 47% of global responsible AI specialists despite being 36% of total AI talent.

### 5. Klarna (Sweden) — The Hybrid Pivot

**Chronology and arc:**
Klarna is the most instructive case because it shows the full arc: aggressive automation, a public correction, and a deliberate pivot to hybrid. In early 2024, Klarna's AI assistant handled 2.3 million conversations in its first month — equivalent to 700 agents. Resolution time dropped from 11 minutes to under 2. CEO Sebastian Siemiatkowski projected $40 million in annual profit improvement. Since 2022, headcount dropped from 5,527 to ~2,907, primarily through attrition and a hiring freeze — not layoffs.

Then the correction. By mid-2025, Siemiatkowski publicly acknowledged the strategy *"went too far."* Cost-cutting as the dominant evaluation factor led to lower quality. Klarna began re-hiring human agents for complex and emotional cases. The hybrid model — AI for simple tasks, humans for complex/emotional — is now the declared endgame.
*(Sources: Time, May 2025; Entrepreneur, 2025; Bloomberg, May 2025; Yahoo Finance, 2025; The Guardian, November 2025)*

**Dilemma — Scale vs. quality; cost vs. experience:**
Klarna's dilemma was the most dramatic: how far can you push AI before customers push back? The answer: further than most companies dare, but not as far as full replacement. Siemiatkowski: *"As cost unfortunately seems to have been a too predominant evaluation factor when organizing this, what you end up having is lower quality."*

**Metrics (with sources and dates):**
- AI assistant: 2.3 million conversations/month (≈700 agents equivalent) *(Klarna, 2024)*
- Resolution time: 11 min → <2 min *(Klarna, 2024)*
- $40M projected annual profit improvement *(Klarna, 2024)*
- Workforce: 5,527 → ~2,907 (2022–2025) *(The Guardian, November 2025)*
- Revenue per employee: $575,000 → $1.1 million (152% increase since Q1 2023) *(Finextra; TechCrunch, 2025)*
- Average employee compensation increased 60%: $126,000 → $203,000 *(The Guardian, 2025)*
- 96% of employees use AI daily *(Finextra, 2025)*
- Tech employees as share of workforce: 36% (2022) → 52% (Q1 2025) *(Finextra, 2025)*
- AI now performs work equivalent to ~853 full-time employees *(The Guardian, 2025)*

**The hybrid model:**
Klarna now positions human support as "VIP treatment" — AI handles routine tasks while humans manage complex issues and relationship-building. The company is testing a gig-model setup ("Uber-type arrangement") for remote customer service representatives. Siemiatkowski: *"I just think it's so critical that you are clear to your customer that there will be always a human if you want."*

**Leadership quotes:**
- Sebastian Siemiatkowski: *"AI gives us speed. Talent gives us empathy."*
- Siemiatkowski: *"Two things can be true at the same time"* — AI automates manual work while humans provide premium service.
- Siemiatkowski: *"As cost unfortunately seems to have been a too predominant evaluation factor... what you end up having is lower quality."*
- 86% of customers prefer empathy over speed for complex issues *(Klarna survey data)*

**European angle:**
Sweden/Nordic context; strong labour market norms; Klarna's correction validates the European instinct for workforce dialogue — the companies that involved employees early (ING, Allianz) avoided the public reversal Klarna experienced.

### 6. Schneider Electric (France) — AI-Powered Industrial Transformation

**Chronology and strategy:**
Schneider Electric appointed Philippe Rambach as its first Chief AI Officer in November 2021 and created a global AI Hub. The AI Hub reports directly to executive leadership and focuses on three areas: (1) technology platforms and analytics; (2) process guidance and expertise; (3) partnerships with data platform owners for data federation. In May 2025, Schneider announced a landmark multi-year initiative building an AI-native ecosystem for sustainability and energy management. In January 2026, it launched Resource Advisor+, an energy and sustainability intelligence platform with "frugal AI by design."
*(Sources: TED Magazine; Schneider Electric press; Schneider Electric blog, 2025–2026)*

**Frugal AI — the distinctive philosophy:**
Schneider Electric's signature approach is "frugal AI" — solving problems with the smallest effective solution rather than defaulting to large language models. The company collaborated with 140 organisations on Spec 2314 for measuring and reducing AI's environmental impact. Three principles: (1) build safety that drives efficiency; (2) deploy workloads at lower carbon sites; (3) right-size every model. Rambach: *"not using a sledgehammer to hit a nail."*
*(Source: Diginomica; Schneider Electric blog)*

**Metrics (with sources and dates):**
- 6-day reduction in inventory days *(existing research)*
- 15% average yield improvement on specific lines *(existing research)*
- Over €100 million in generated value *(existing research)*
- Target: €2.0–2.5 billion in cumulative industrial productivity gains (2026–2030) *(existing research)*
- 30% inventory savings via multi-echelon optimisation vs. 11% from traditional methods *(Supply Chain 24/7)*

**Dilemma — Industrial reliability vs. AI adoption; sustainability vs. scale:**
Schneider's unique dilemma is at the intersection of industrial reliability and sustainability. AI cannot be deployed recklessly in energy management and industrial automation where failures have real-world consequences. The frugal AI approach resolves this: right-sized models, edge deployment, carbon-aware computing.

**Trust Charter and responsible AI:**
Schneider operates under a Trust Charter — its code of conduct governing all operations, updated March 2025, available in 30+ languages. The Trust Charter covers four pillars: trust with customers, trusted teams, trust with investors, and trust with communities. AI governance is embedded within this charter, requiring executive oversight, internal governance, and human oversight throughout the product lifecycle.
*(Sources: Schneider Electric Trust Charter; Schneider Electric blog, 2025)*

**Leadership quotes:**
- Philippe Rambach on frugal AI: Emphasises *"not using a sledgehammer to hit a nail"* — maximum intelligence with minimal resource consumption.
- Rambach advocates for edge and embedded AI — reducing latency, lowering costs, improving energy efficiency, and strengthening cybersecurity through localised processing.

**European angle:**
France; President Macron's €109 billion AI infrastructure commitment; Schneider's frugal AI aligns with European sustainability values; Trust Charter echoes EU AI Act's trust-first approach.

## The 9 Transferable Lessons

### Lesson 1: CEO Ownership Must Be Visible and Sustained, Not Delegated at the First Milestone

**Lesson statement:** The CEO and board must personally champion AI transformation — not just approve the budget but visibly use the tools, train alongside employees, and hold the agenda. Delegation to IT or a project team is the single most reliable predictor of stalled transformation.

**Cases that illustrate it:**
- **BBVA:** Chairman Torres Vila and CEO Genç were among the 250 senior leaders personally trained on ChatGPT Enterprise. The rollout was a board-level decision from the start.
- **ING:** The CEO sits on the AI Ethics Committee reviewing each use case. COO van Stiphout personally drives the AI agenda.
- **Klarna:** Siemiatkowski drove the AI strategy from the top — both the initial push and the public correction. His willingness to reverse course publicly was itself a leadership act.
- **Schneider Electric:** Appointed a Chief AI Officer reporting directly to executive leadership — signalling that AI is not an IT function.

**Evidence/quote for the Writer:**
Torres Vila: *"We were pioneers in the digital and mobile transformation, and we are now entering the AI era with even greater ambition."* — This is a chairman speaking as owner of the transformation, not as sponsor of a project.

---

### Lesson 2: Govern First, Experiment Second — Set the Guardrails Before You Scale

**Lesson statement:** The companies that scale fastest are not the ones that experiment most freely — they are the ones that build governance, risk controls, and compliance into the foundation before they launch. Governance is an accelerator, not a brake.

**Cases that illustrate it:**
- **ING:** Van Stiphout's "conservatively aggressive" model — centralised governance, standardised platforms, real-time monitoring — enabled a 90% pilot-to-production rate. *"Without this governance... we might have seen bits of AI all over the place."*
- **Allianz:** Signed the EU AI Pact (November 2024); Anthropic partnership co-develops auditable systems; 12,000 employees completed AI upskilling within a governance framework.
- **BBVA:** Security, legal, and compliance teams aligned from day one of the ChatGPT Enterprise rollout — not brought in after the pilot.
- **Schneider Electric:** Trust Charter governs all AI, requiring executive oversight and human oversight throughout the lifecycle.

**Evidence/quote for the Writer:**
Van Stiphout: *"Without this governance... we might have seen bits of AI all over the place."* Contrast with the 30% industry average pilot-to-production rate. ING's governance didn't slow them down — it is the reason they are 3x faster than competitors.

---

### Lesson 3: Build a Central AI Engine, Then Embed Expertise Where Value Is Created

**Lesson statement:** Successful companies build centralised AI capability (a factory, hub, or centre of excellence) to set standards, governance, and infrastructure — then embed specialists directly in business units to ensure AI solves real business problems.

**Cases that illustrate it:**
- **BBVA:** AI Factory (~400 professionals in Spain, Mexico, Turkey) builds the platform; ~300 "AI wizards" are embedded in business units. Now 1,000+ data scientists across the organisation.
- **ING:** Centralised platform across five strategic domains, with COO oversight — but AI deployed through local hubs across countries. Head of advanced analytics: centralised platform enabled scaling across multiple countries while maintaining business connections.
- **Siemens:** Industrial Copilot developed centrally (with NVIDIA, Microsoft, Accenture partnerships), deployed to individual factories. Nanjing and Amberg are local instantiations of a central platform.
- **Allianz:** Central governance and ~400 GenAI use cases, but Project Nemo was developed locally in Australia and is being scaled to other markets.

**Evidence/quote for the Writer:**
BBVA's structure is the clearest illustration: AI Factory (central engine) + AI wizards (embedded). The AI Factory website describes it as "transforming AI opportunities into data products addressing client needs by collaborating closely with business units." The hub-and-spoke model appears in every successful case.

### Lesson 4: Measure What the Board Cares About — Tie Every AI Initiative to a Business KPI

**Lesson statement:** The leaders do not measure AI activity (number of pilots, models deployed, tokens processed). They measure business outcomes: time saved, revenue generated, cost reduced, quality improved. Every AI initiative has a business KPI before it launches.

**Cases that illustrate it:**
- **BBVA:** 3 hours/week saved per employee; 87% reduction in query handling time; 83% weekly active usage — all tied to productivity.
- **ING:** 25% productivity gains; 90% pilot-to-production rate; 75% customer queries automated — operational KPIs.
- **Siemens:** 200% capacity increase; 78% lead time reduction; 46% fewer field failures; 28% carbon reduction — manufacturing KPIs.
- **Allianz:** 80% reduction in claims processing time; Project Nemo under 100 days to build — speed and efficiency KPIs.
- **Klarna:** Revenue per employee from $575K to $1.1M; resolution time from 11 min to <2 min; $40M profit impact.
- **Schneider Electric:** 6-day inventory reduction; 15% yield improvement; €100M+ in generated value; €2.0–2.5B target.

**Evidence/quote for the Writer:**
Allianz's Manuela Diviach captures this: the company maintains *"a disciplined approach to prioritising innovative AI projects, guided by the core principle of value creation."* None of these leaders measure AI for AI's sake.

### Lesson 5: Close the Pilot-to-Production Gap — 90% Deployed, Not 90% Piloted

**Lesson statement:** The industry average is 30% of AI pilots reaching production. The leaders achieve 90%. The difference is not better technology — it is organisational readiness: clear criteria for go/no-go, centralised platforms, pre-aligned governance, and executive accountability for scaling.

**Cases that illustrate it:**
- **ING:** 90% pilot-to-production — the benchmark. Achieved through centralised platform, standardised cloud-hosted models, and five clearly defined strategic domains.
- **BBVA:** Phased rollout (3,000 → 11,000 → 120,000) demonstrates systematic scaling. Each phase had clear success criteria before expanding.
- **Allianz:** Project Nemo built in under 100 days and immediately designated for expansion to other claim types. The modular, scalable design was baked in from the start.
- **Siemens:** Nanjing factory is now a WEF Lighthouse — not a pilot. Fifth Siemens site to achieve Lighthouse status. The pattern is: prove in one factory, then replicate.

**Evidence/quote for the Writer:**
ING's 90% vs. the industry's 30% is the single most powerful data point in this chapter. It proves that the bottleneck is governance and organisational readiness, not technology. The Writer should use this as the anchor for the lesson.

### Lesson 6: The Hybrid Human–AI Model Is the Endgame

**Lesson statement:** Full automation is not the goal. The leaders have learned — some painfully — that the endgame is humans and AI working together. AI handles volume, speed, and routine; humans handle complexity, empathy, and judgment. The companies that got this right from the start avoided costly reversals.

**Cases that illustrate it:**
- **Klarna:** The defining case. Went all-in on AI automation (2.3M conversations/month, ~700 agents replaced). Then publicly reversed course: *"cost unfortunately seems to have been a too predominant evaluation factor... what you end up having is lower quality."* Now positions human support as "VIP treatment."
- **Allianz:** Project Nemo uses 7 AI agents but keeps humans in the final decision-making loop. Designed hybrid from the start.
- **ING:** "Human in the loop" is standard practice. AI assists, humans decide.
- **Siemens:** Industrial Copilot assists engineers — it does not replace them. Neike's vision is the AI-augmented expert, not the autonomous factory.

**Evidence/quote for the Writer:**
Siemiatkowski: *"AI gives us speed. Talent gives us empathy."* And: *"I just think it's so critical that you are clear to your customer that there will be always a human if you want."* The 86% customer preference for empathy over speed in complex issues is the data point that seals it. Klarna's arc (automate → correct → hybrid) is a powerful narrative for the chapter.

### Lesson 7: Turn Regulatory Compliance Into Competitive Moat

**Lesson statement:** European leaders do not treat the EU AI Act as a cost of doing business. They treat compliance as capability-building — governance infrastructure, trust, auditability, and data quality that become competitive advantages in markets where customers and regulators increasingly demand them.

**Cases that illustrate it:**
- **Allianz:** Signed the EU AI Pact (November 2024). Anthropic partnership (January 2026) co-develops systems logging every decision, rationale, and data source. European insurers hold 47% of global responsible AI specialists.
- **ING:** Governance-first approach aligns with EU AI Act expectations. The compliance discipline enables, rather than constrains, the 90% production rate.
- **BBVA:** Co-developed with IBM a stress test for discriminatory bias in GenAI models for non-English languages, presented at NeurIPS — compliance-driven research producing genuine innovation.
- **Schneider Electric:** Trust Charter governs all operations. Frugal AI aligns with EU sustainability values. Responsible AI embedded in product design.

**Evidence/quote for the Writer:**
The Accenture finding: "AI Achievers" — the 11% of European firms generating 50% greater revenue growth from AI — share a defining practice: *"designing AI responsibly from the start, not as an afterthought."* Allianz's EU AI Pact signing, BBVA's NeurIPS bias research, and ING's governance-enabled scaling all prove the thesis: compliance is not a brake, it is a moat.

### Lesson 8: Move Fast — Build in Weeks, Deploy in Months

**Lesson statement:** Governance does not mean slow. The leaders build and deploy at remarkable speed. The secret: standardised platforms, centralised tooling, clear decision criteria, and pre-aligned compliance mean teams can move fast within clear boundaries.

**Cases that illustrate it:**
- **Allianz:** Project Nemo built and deployed in under 100 days — from concept to live claims processing.
- **BBVA:** Phased rollout from 3,000 to 120,000 employees within months. The OpenAI partnership announcement and full-scale deployment happened within the same year.
- **ING:** Centralised platform enables deployment across multiple countries simultaneously. Customer due diligence that once took weeks now takes seconds.
- **Siemens:** Nanjing factory achieved WEF Lighthouse status. Lead times cut by 78%; time-to-market reduced by 33% (45-day to 10-day delivery windows). AI agents announced May 2025 already in pre-release.

**Evidence/quote for the Writer:**
Allianz's Project Nemo — under 100 days from concept to production — is the best speed exemplar. It directly counters the perception that European companies are slow or that governance slows deployment. ING's due-diligence acceleration (weeks → seconds) and Siemens' 78% lead-time cut add further evidence.

### Lesson 9: Reinvest AI Savings Into Your People, Not Just Your P&L

**Lesson statement:** The leaders reinvest AI-driven efficiency gains into their workforce — through higher compensation, upskilling, new roles, and career development. This creates a virtuous cycle: employees embrace AI because it visibly benefits them, which accelerates adoption, which generates more value.

**Cases that illustrate it:**
- **Klarna:** Average employee compensation increased 60% ($126K → $203K). Revenue per employee rose to $1.1M. Tech employees as share of workforce: 36% → 52%. Savings reinvested into remaining staff's salaries and equity.
- **BBVA:** Surpassed 1,000 data scientists — grew the AI workforce rather than just cutting headcount. 250 senior leaders trained. 20,000+ custom GPTs created by employees shows bottom-up engagement.
- **ING:** 5,000 employees trained on data fluency and GenAI. Employees interact with AI 30–50 times per week — active adoption, not passive compliance.
- **Allianz:** 12,000 employees completed AI upskilling in 2024. Internal ChatGPT instance with responsible framework and prompt library. Karuth-Zelle: success requires *"sharing mindset, curiosity, creativity, and can-do attitude."*

**Evidence/quote for the Writer:**
Klarna's 60% pay increase is the headline number. Siemiatkowski acknowledged the company went too far in cutting people; the reinvestment into remaining employees is the correction that makes the hybrid model work. BBVA's 1,000+ data scientists and ING's 5,000 trained employees show that the best companies grow their AI-capable workforce alongside their AI capability.
