### Finding 7
- **Query:** EU AI Act Article 13 transparency requirements product design compliance 2026
- **Claim or quote:** EU AI Act Article 13 requires high-risk AI systems to be designed for sufficient transparency so deployers can interpret outputs and use them appropriately. Providers must supply documentation on intended purpose, accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity characteristics, limitations, human oversight measures, and logging mechanisms. Conformity assessments, CE marking, and EU database registration for high-risk AI products must be completed by August 2026.
- **Source:** EU AI Act Service Desk, Article 13; SecurePrivacy, "EU AI Act 2026 Compliance Guide"
- **URL:** https://ai-act-service-desk.ec.europa.eu/en/ai-act/article-13; https://secureprivacy.ai/blog/eu-ai-act-2026-compliance
- **Use for:** European context beat — compliance obligations that affect product design decisions, especially for high-risk categories (credit, insurance, employment, healthcare).

### Finding 8
- **Query:** EU AI Act Article 13 transparency requirements product design compliance 2026
- **Claim or quote:** Limited-risk AI systems (e.g., chatbots, deepfakes, AI-generated content) must notify users they are interacting with AI under Article 52 of the EU AI Act. These transparency obligations cover a broad category of consumer-facing AI products — not only high-risk systems — and apply from August 2026.
- **Source:** LegalNodes, "EU AI Act 2026 Updates: Compliance Requirements and Business Risks," 2026
- **URL:** https://www.legalnodes.com/article/eu-ai-act-2026-updates-compliance-requirements-and-business-risks
- **Use for:** European context — product teams building customer-facing AI features must plan disclosure and transparency into design, not retrofit it.
