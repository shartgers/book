## Findings

### Finding 1
- **Query:** CEO AI vision versus AI strategy difference leadership 2025 2026
- **Claim or quote:** Almost three-quarters (75%) of CEOs are now their organisation's main decision-maker on AI strategy. Yet fewer than 30% report seeing measurable ROI from their AI investments — confirming the gap between vision ownership and results.
- **Source:** Conference Board / USAII / World Economic Forum synthesis, January 2026
- **URL:** https://www.conference-board.org/research/ced-policy-backgrounders/ai-and-the-c-suite-implications-for-ceo-strategy-in-2026
- **Use for:** Opening hook — CEOs own the vision declaration but many lack the translation mechanism to convert it into measurable strategic objectives

### Finding 2
- **Query:** CEO AI vision versus AI strategy difference leadership 2025 2026
- **Claim or quote:** "CEOs set the AI vision for the company, while CIOs apply knowledge of every part of the organisation to build the processes that realise the vision." The vision/execution boundary is the most common point of failure.
- **Source:** World Economic Forum, November 2025 — "How CEOs and CIOs can lead the AI transformation together"
- **URL:** https://www.weforum.org/stories/2025/11/how-ceos-and-cios-can-lead-the-ai-transformation-together/
- **Use for:** Vision vs. strategy distinction; why vision must precede and frame strategy; CEO-CIO role boundary

### Finding 3
- **Query:** CEO AI vision versus AI strategy difference leadership 2025 2026
- **Claim or quote:** By end of 2026, nearly 75% of CEOs globally expect AI to materially impact their business model — yet fewer than 30% report measurable ROI from AI investments. The gap is a vision-to-execution translation failure, not a technology failure.
- **Source:** EY CEO Outlook 2026 / WEF January 2026
- **URL:** https://www.weforum.org/stories/2026/01/ceos-are-all-in-on-ai-but-anxieties-remain/
- **Use for:** Urgency framing; vision without translation mechanism produces no results

### Finding 4
- **Query:** CEO-owned AI vision failure delegated to CTO CIO transformation 2025
- **Claim or quote:** "AI cannot be delegated to IT alone. This is not about systems and servers, it's about strategy, culture, workforce, and risk. AI belongs in the boardroom, and CEOs must lead from the front." Both symbolically and practically, the CEO must retain direct oversight of the AI Transformation Office as part of the steering committee.
- **Source:** 9altitudes — "AI in the boardroom: why CEOs must lead the transformation", 2025
- **URL:** https://9altitudes.com/insights-events/articles/ai-belongs-in-the-boardroom-why-ceos-must-lead-the-transformation
- **Use for:** What happens when AI vision is delegated to technical teams; CEO ownership imperative

### Finding 5
- **Query:** CEO-owned AI vision failure delegated to CTO CIO transformation 2025
- **Claim or quote:** 70% of CEOs are involved in shaping their organisation's AI strategy, but their engagement significantly decreases in key areas: identifying use cases (32%), evaluating technologies (26%), developing tools (18%), and reskilling workers (13%). Vision is declared at the top; execution is abandoned in the middle.
- **Source:** BizTech Magazine / WEF synthesis, 2025
- **URL:** https://www.weforum.org/stories/2025/11/how-ceos-and-cios-can-lead-the-ai-transformation-together/
- **Use for:** The "vision abandonment cliff" — CEOs who set vision but disengage at execution; why vision must include measurable objectives

### Finding 6
- **Query:** BBVA DBS AI vision strategy CEO leadership values governance 2025
- **Claim or quote:** BBVA Chair Carlos Torres Vila and Sam Altman jointly announced a strategic alliance aimed at "redefining the future of financial services through the use of artificial intelligence." BBVA has adopted a data and AI governance strategy that keeps an inventory of AI systems and monitors them regularly for accuracy, errors, and currency. 250 senior leaders — including the chairman and CEO — learned hands-on AI capabilities to drive adoption.
- **Source:** BBVA.com / OpenAI case study, 2025
- **URL:** https://openai.com/index/bbva-2025/
- **Use for:** BBVA as CEO-led vision with governance backbone; the chairman learning alongside staff as a values signal

### Finding 7
- **Query:** BBVA DBS AI vision strategy CEO leadership values governance 2025
- **Claim or quote:** DBS's transformation is powered by "an early and unwavering strategic vision from leadership, the development of proprietary in-house technology platforms, the cultivation of a deeply embedded data-driven culture, and a robust governance framework that actively enables, rather than hinders, rapid innovation." A cross-functional Responsible AI Council — composed of senior leaders from legal, risk, and technology — oversees and approves AI use cases.
- **Source:** Klover.ai — "DBS's AI Strategy: Analysis of Dominance in Financial Services AI", 2025
- **URL:** https://www.klover.ai/dbs-ai-strategy-analysis-of-dominance-in-financial-services-ai/
- **Use for:** DBS as gold-standard vision case; the Responsible AI Council as values-governance instrument; "unwavering" as the key characteristic of CEO-owned vision

### Finding 8
- **Query:** DBS Bank GANDALF AI vision Piyush Gupta strategy leadership values
- **Claim or quote:** GANDALF (Google, Amazon, Netflix, Apple, LinkedIn, Facebook) was adopted as DBS's cultural benchmark — the CIO created the mnemonic to guide DBS in becoming a technology company by studying technology companies, "leaving an opening for DBS to become the letter 'D'." The vision was not to win against other banks but to compete with tech giants.
- **Source:** MIT Sloan Management Review — "Portrait of an AI Leader: Piyush Gupta of DBS Bank"
- **URL:** https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/portrait-an-ai-leader-piyush-gupta-dbs-bank/
- **Use for:** What a CEO-level AI vision looks like in practice: reframing the competitive set entirely, not just adopting technology; GANDALF as a vision instrument

### Finding 9
- **Query:** DBS Bank GANDALF AI vision Piyush Gupta strategy leadership values
- **Claim or quote:** Piyush Gupta championed the AI initiative, ensuring AI became embedded in the bank's DNA, creating the cultural shift necessary for adoption across all business units. His vision: "make banking joyful" by rendering it invisible and effortless for the customer. DBS deployed over 1,500 AI/ML models across 370+ distinct use cases by early 2025, generating S$750M+ in AI economic value in 2024.
- **Source:** easyglobalbanking.com / Klover.ai synthesis, 2025
- **URL:** https://www.easyglobalbanking.com/singapore-banking/singapore-ai-banking/
- **Use for:** DBS case data — vision translated into measurable objectives; "make banking joyful" as a human-centred vision statement that drives AI strategy

### Finding 10
- **Query:** EU AI Act board governance AI vision values obligations CEO accountability 2025 2026
- **Claim or quote:** Boards must verify that management has identified all AI systems, classified them by risk level, and assigned accountability — demanding documented governance structures, vendor compliance evidence, and oversight workflows. Fiduciary duty now explicitly extends to AI oversight; the era of "passive awareness" is over.
- **Source:** WilmerHale — "Board Oversight and Artificial Intelligence: Key Governance Priorities for 2026", January 2026
- **URL:** https://www.wilmerhale.com/en/insights/client-alerts/20260122-board-oversight-and-artificial-intelligence-key-governance-priorities-for-2026
- **Use for:** Board's role in stress-testing the Vision Layer; fiduciary duty as the governance anchor

### Finding 11
- **Query:** Board stress testing AI strategy vision governance fiduciary duty 2025 2026
- **Claim or quote:** Organisations with board-level AI governance frameworks achieve 55% higher ROI on AI investments than those without. Trust is demonstrated with scorecards: explainability, fairness, resilience, auditability, intervention readiness, and evidence of consistent behaviour under stress.
- **Source:** Various board governance sources synthesised across NACD, WilmerHale, thinking.inc, 2025–2026
- **URL:** https://www.wilmerhale.com/en/insights/client-alerts/20260122-board-oversight-and-artificial-intelligence-key-governance-priorities-for-2026
- **Use for:** Board stress-testing the Vision Layer; governance ROI data point; values not as posters but as stress-tested commitments
