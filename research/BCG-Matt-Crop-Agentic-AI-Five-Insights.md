## Key Insight 1: Think Exponentials, Not Linears

**Quote:** *"Any sufficiently advanced technology is indistinguishable from magic."*

- Humans think in linear extrapolations; AI grows exponentially.
- We will always be surprised by how capable AI becomes.
- **Example:** 2021 DALL·E avocado chairs → 2025 photorealistic AI video in four years.
- **Situational awareness paper (Ashen Brener, OpenAI):** Three drivers—more compute, model efficiency, and "unhobbling" (step changes in how we build/use models)—could deliver **10,000x more power in three years**.
- With that trajectory: models at **PhD-level expertise on every topic** within a few years.
- **Implication:** Plan for the future, not today's demos. Don't dismiss AI because "it didn't work yesterday."

---

## Key Insight 2: Agentic AI = Observe, Plan, Act, Iterate

**Definition:** Systems that can observe, plan, take actions with tools, and iterate.

- Shift from **rigid, rules-based workflows** to **reasoning-driven workflows** powered by LLMs.
- **Future architecture:** An AI agent layer sits between humans and systems of record.
- Humans become **orchestrators** of fleets of agents rather than clicking through rigid workflows.
- Agents interact with systems of record, pull data, take action; humans give instructions and oversee.

## Key Insight 3: Three Waves of GenAI Adoption

| Phase | Year | Focus |
|-------|------|-------|
| Experiments | 2023 | No budget, lots of learning |
| Thousand flowers | 2024 | Every department had GenAI initiatives; many chatbots; little impact |
| Big rocks | 2025+ | Focus on a few high-ROI transformations |

- **IT spend:** Overall softening (projected 4% → 2.4% growth), but **AI/ML still growing**.
- Companies now ask: *"How do we get impact on the bottom line?"*
- **BCG "Future Built" research:** AI-leading companies get **2x ROI** on AI projects and **1.5–2x total shareholder return**. Most companies are still early.

---

## Key Insight 4: Three Value Plays—Deploy, Reshape, Invent

1. **Deploy:** Roll out AI-powered tools (ChatGPT, Copilot, Salesforce Agent Force, etc.). Requires training and process change.
2. **Reshape:** Redesign end-to-end processes. Automate toil; keep high-value human work. Not just deploying a chatbot—rethinking the whole workflow.
3. **Invent:** Build new offerings using AI capabilities (natural language, world knowledge, creativity, reason). Winners will be determined here.

- **Laggards** spend most effort on deploy. **Leaders** spend more on reshape and invent.

## Key Insight 5: Beyond Cost—Four Dimensions of AI Value

| Dimension | What it means |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Reduce toil** | Automate manual, low-value work (cost reduction) |
| **Boost productivity** | Make human time more productive (e.g., developer writes 10x more code in same time) |
| **Improve quality** | AI raises output quality |
| **Surpass human scale** | Do things that were too costly with humans alone (e.g., nurse follow-up calls) |

- **750 knowledge workers study (BCG, Harvard, Wharton, MIT):** With AI: **25% faster**, **40% better quality**, **bottom performers matched top performers**.
- Don't frame AI only as headcount reduction.

## Software Development: Leading Edge of Agentic Adoption

- **GitHub Copilot at a major bank (10,000 engineers):** Top 5% **doubled productivity** in four months. 60% had **zero impact**—because they didn't use the tool.
- **Five-level ladder of automation:**
  - **Level 0:** Refuse to use AI
  - **Level 1:** Chat/answer questions (5% lift)
  - **Level 2:** Write some code with AI (10–20% AI-written; ~20% lift)
  - **Level 3:** Prompt for features; 50% AI-generated code
  - **Level 4:** "Vibe coder"—architect and prompt; agent writes code. *"I'm never going back."*

- **Adoption metric:** Logging in once a month ≠ adoption. Measure % of AI-generated code or equivalent.
- **Silicon Valley / Y Combinator startups:** Already at Level 4. Future competitors will write code 10–50x faster.

## Human Resistance: Three Root Causes

1. **Belief:** "I don't know the tool can do it" or "I tried once and it failed."
2. **Habits:** "This is how I write a job description." Stuck in old workflows.
3. **Identity:** "I'm a coder. I write code. Who am I if AI does it?" Hardest to address.

- **Reframe:** AI elevates people from typists to architects. Focus on what elevates the human.

---

## Six Levers for Adoption

1. **Right tool:** Integrated, in the right place, working (e.g., overeager security filters blocking every third response kills adoption).
2. **Explicit instruction:** Tell people how it works and how to apply it to their job.
3. **Measure adoption early:** Use meaningful metrics (e.g., % AI-generated code).
4. **Celebrate champions:** Make early adopters visible; they become navigators.
5. **Ramp scarcity:** Expect more output; take away resources so there's incentive to use tools.
6. **Minimize toil, maximize joy:** Redesign work so automation removes the parts people dislike; keep the parts they value.

- **Example:** Equity research chatbot summarizing reports → max 2 FTE impact. Instead, map 100 activities, separate toil from joy, automate toil, keep joy.

## Five C-Suite Questions

1. **Where do I want to create value?** Right use cases, functions, big rocks.
2. **Where can I fund the journey?** What pays back to fund more development?
3. **Do I have the right technology infrastructure?** Data, cloud, vendors, platforms.
4. **Do I have a focused change program?** Technology + business; adoption is central.
5. **Do I have the right guardrails?** Deploy AI responsibly.
