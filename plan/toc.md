## Table of Contents

### Part I: The Burning Platform

**Chapter 1: The Largest Shift Since the Industrial Revolution** (9%)
Why this moment is different: the paradigm shift, the 95% failure rate, and why the window for first-mover advantage is narrowing.

**Chapter 2: Leadership Is Not Optional** (8%)
Why transformation fails when delegated: the roles of CEO, C-suite, and board; shared accountability as a prerequisite for success.

### Part II: The Model

**Chapter 3: What the Agentic Enterprise Looks Like** (13%)
A working definition of the Agentic Enterprise and its five elements: Products, People, Process, Technology, and Data. Introduces the **agentic employee** (the human half of the equation) and the three emerging profiles (supervisor, specialist, augmented frontline worker). Defines the mindset shift from task-doer to orchestrator and why organisations must redesign roles, not just adopt tools.

**Chapter 4: The Four Tiers of Transformation** (11%)
How transformation progresses from Individual to Team to Department to Organisation, and what leadership must do differently at each tier. The Individual tier is where agentic employees are made: AI fluency, skill creation and sharing, and the compounding network effect that turns one person's innovation into organisational capability.

**Chapter 5: Value, Capabilities, Trust** (9%)
The three-angle decision framework for evaluating every AI investment and setting the right priorities.

### Part III: The European Advantage

**Chapter 6: Europe's Distinctive Edge** (8%)
How regulation, trust, and pragmatism become competitive advantages rather than constraints, and what the EU AI Act demands of leadership now.

**Chapter 7: What the Leaders Did** (15%)
Deep case studies from BBVA, ING, Siemens, Allianz, Klarna, and Schneider Electric: the dilemmas, decisions, and transferable lessons.

### Part IV: The CEO's Playbook

**Chapter 8: Building Your Agentic Enterprise** (13%)
A phased approach: Foundation (governance, data readiness, AI literacy) → Activation (Centre of Excellence, pilots, first wins) → Scale (cross-functional transformation, operating model redesign). Includes practical guidance on developing agentic employees at every phase: from embedded learning and skill-sharing incentives in Foundation, to blended human-agent teams in Activation, to full workforce-level agentic capability at Scale.

**Chapter 9: Foundations of the AI transformation** (8%)
Understanding what must be in place before and alongside transformation: regulatory and ethical context (EU AI Act, trustworthy AI), the five pillars (Product & Strategy, People & Skills, Processes & Governance, Technology & Infrastructure, Data & Context), and the Value–Capability–Trust lenses. How to run a structured readiness diagnostic, score maturity, and turn the result into a phased roadmap with clear milestones and KPIs so the organisation can absorb and act on AI rather than deploy it in isolation.

**Chapter 10: Measuring What Matters** (8%)
The KPIs that reveal real progress, how to avoid pilot purgatory, and the board's evolving governance role.

### Conclusion

**Chapter 11: A leadership decision** (6%)
The choice every leadership team faces. The cost of waiting. What the Agentic Enterprise looks like three years from now, and who is building it today.
